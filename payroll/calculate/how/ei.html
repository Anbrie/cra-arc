Calculate EI deductions

How to use the EI premiums tables and how to calculate manually the amount to withhold.

  • Calculation methods
  • References

Calculation methods

Calculate EI premiums using one of the following methods:

  • Tax tables (regular payments)
  • Bonuses, retroactive payments or irregular amounts
  • Manual calculation
Tax tables (regular payments)

Reminder: There are online calculators that will calculate the EI deductions for you.

What are "EI premium tables"

EI premiums table are [description here].

Steps

  1. Determine if you can use EI premiums tables

    EI premiums tables can be used in most situations.

    When to use or not to use the tax tables

    Use when

    • The payment is made with regular pay
    • You received Form TD1-IN (in addition to the TD1) from your employee, only use these tables on the portion of their income which is taxable with the claims information reported on the TD1

    Do not use when

    • The payment (for example, bonuses, commissions, retroactive pay or other types of regular payments) is made separately from regular pay
    • The payment is for deductions in a single period, multiple periods, or year-end verification
    • The employee reached the EI maximum contributions for the year
    • You received Form TD1X from your employee
    • You received Form TD3F from the individual, refer to: Fishers and employment insurance

    The annual basic exemption  is built into the EI tables.

    If they have not reached the maximum, continue to next step

Bonuses, retroactive payments or irregular amounts

In addition to regular types of payments, you must deduct EI premiums from bonuses, retroactive payments or other types of irregular payments.

Steps

  1. Determine if you should can the bonus method

    • Use the bonus method

      If the payment is made separately from regular pay, use the bonus method to calculate manually because no basic exemption is included

    • Do not use the bonus method

      If the payment is made with regular pay, use either:

      • Payroll Deductions Online Calculator (PDOC)
      • Calculations of CPP, EI and income tax deductions using the tax tables

  3. Calculation

    • Bonus, retroactive or irregular payment
    • xmultiply by EI rate 
    • =equals This amount is the EI contribution that you should withhold from your employee's pay. You have to remit this amount and a matching amount for your share of the EI contribution.
    Example
    • Joseph receives a retroactive pay increase of $450.00 on June 29 2021
    • xmultiply by EI rate is 5.45%
    • =equals $24.53 is the amount of EI contributions you should deduct. Your equal share of EI contributions is $24.53. The total amount to remit is $49.06.
