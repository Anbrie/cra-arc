Applying for an individual tax number (ITN)

An individual tax number (ITN) is a 9-digit number issued to non-resident individuals who need an identification number but who are not eligible to obtain a social insurance number (SIN).

Who can apply

You can apply for an ITN if you are a non-resident who is not eligible to obtain a SIN.

You will need to provide an ITN to the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) if you are a non-resident who needs to:

  • File an application to waive or reduce Canadian withholding tax
  • Dispose of taxable Canadian property
  • File a Canadian Income Tax Return
  • File a Canadian Income Tax Return for Election under section 216

International students

Without a work permit

International students without a work permit will need to provide an ITN to the CRA when filing a Canadian Income Tax Return.

With a work permit

International students with a work permit should contact Service Canada directly to get a SIN.

Sole proprietors

If you are a sole proprietor, you may also have to apply for a business number (BN).

For more information, visit How to register for a business number or Canada Revenue Agency program accounts.

If you are eligible to obtain a SIN, contact Service Canada.

Apply for a Social Insurance Number

If you have previously obtained a SIN, an ITN, or a temporary taxation number (TTN), continue to use the tax number you have already been issued.

When to apply

Apply for your ITN as early as possible to make sure you receive it before you need to use it.

How to apply

Fill out Form T1261, Application for a Canada Revenue Agency Individual Tax Number (ITN) for Non-Residents

  • To avoid processing delays, you must select the applicable box under "Indicate the reason you are applying for an ITN"
  • Complete sections 1 to 4 in the "Identification" section
  • Print your name, and provide the date and your signature in the "Certification of Applicant" section

Include supporting documents

Include the original, certified, or notarized copies of your supporting documents with your form.

We will return any original documents that you submit. We will not return copies of documents.

The supporting documents must:

  • Be current and verify your identity
  • Include your name, date of birth, and a photograph
  • Be accompanied by an official translation if in a language other than English or French
Examples of acceptable supporting documents
  • Passport
  • Driver's license
  • Study permit/ student authorization
  • Diplomatic identity card
    • Certified or notarized copies of documents

    A certified or notarized copy is a photocopy of a document, which is then duly signed with a notation that this is a true copy of the original.

    The person certifying the document must indicate his or her official title, address and contact number.

    • Make sure that these photocopies are certified by a professional such as a public notary, lawyer, doctor or chartered professional accountant (please include membership identification) with an original (ink) signature.
    • The supporting document with the original certification must be included with the ITN application. Photocopies, faxes, or emails with scanned copies of the certified supporting documentation will not be accepted.
    • The certification should appear directly on the supporting documents. If the certification is to appear on a separate page, it must reference the items that are being certified.

    After you apply

    Allow 4 to 6 weeks for us to notify you in writing of your ITN. If you have not received your ITN at the end of the 6-week period, you may contact us to find out the status of your application.
