Make corrections after you remit (pay)

What to do if you under-remitted or over-remitted.

Select the remitting (payment) error

You paid the CRA too little (under-remitted)

You may notice, during the year or when you prepare your information returns, that you have remitted less than you should have. You must remit the required amount immediately. For more information on which method to use, see How to remit (pay).

You paid too much (over-remitted) - Current year

If you remitted more than you should have, you can use the overpayment towards a future payroll period in the current year, if possible.

You paid too much (over-remitted) - Previous year

If the overpayment is in the previous year, you must provide an explanation to request a transfer or refund by using any of the following methods:

  • My Business Account
  • Represent a Client
  • by telephone

To make your request by mail:

  • attach the explanation to your paper information return
  • send a letter to your tax centre

If there is a difference between your remittance total for the year and the amount reported on the corresponding T4 Summary, you will receive a PD4R notice.

Learn more: Payroll correspondence you may receive from us

Your remittance (payment) was not applied to your account correctly

A misallocated payment is a payment that was posted to an account incorrectly. This type of situation does not fall under remitting errors.

If you discover an error with a payment made through a financial institution. If the payment is still being processed, you should first contact the financial institution to determine if a correction can be made.

Learn more: Resolve an issue with a payment.

You made an error in calculating deductions

You may realize that you have overdeducted or underdeducted, EI, CPP or income tax. As soon as you know that the required amount of was not deducted, you should notify your employee. This type of situation does not fall under remitting errors.

If you want to correct a deduction error or a salary overpayment and:

you have not filed, see Correct deduction errors in the current tax year.
  • you already filed the information return, see Correct deduction errors in a previous tax year.
