Common tax terms

Terms starting with the letter B

Basic personal amount (BPA)
A non-refundable tax credit that can be claimed by all individuals.

Terms starting with the letter C

Calendar year
The dates between January 1st and December 31st that is used for personal income tax purposes.

Terms starting with the letter D

Deductions
Amounts that your employer took off of your pay. This includes amounts such as income tax, Canada Pension Plan (CPP) or Quebec Pension Plan (QPP) contributions, and Employment Insurance (EI) premiums.

Terms starting with the letter G

Goods and Services Tax/Harmonized Sales Tax (GST/HST) credit
A tax-free payment that helps individuals and families with low and modest incomes offset the GST or HST that they pay.

Terms starting with the letter I

Income tax and benefit return

The Canadian tax return that individuals complete every year to calculate whether they owe tax on their income. They must also complete a return to receive federal and provincial or territorial benefits and credits.

The process of completing and submitting your tax return to the Canada Revenue Agency is often referred to as "filing your taxes" or "doing your taxes".

Terms starting with the letter M

My Account
The CRA's secure online portal for individuals to view and manage personal income tax and benefit information. You must have filed your taxes for the first time before you can register for this service.

Terms starting with the letter N

Newborn Registration Service
A service used to establish a permanent, legal record of the person’s birth, which is then used for requesting things such as a birth certificate, SIN, or accessing other important services for the child.

Terms starting with the letter T

TD1, Personal Tax Credits Return
A form you must complete when you start a new job so that your employer can pay you accurately. You provide your employer information about your tax situation on this form.
Tax credits
Amounts that help reduce the amount of taxes you owe on your income.
