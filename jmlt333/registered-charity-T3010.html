When to file

To maintain your charitable registration, you must file a complete T3010 return no later than 6 months after the end of your fiscal period.

We recommend signing up for My Business Account (MyBA) as early as possible to be able to file your return online.

If you have never used our digital services before, go to My Business Account for charities for information on how to create an account and start filing your T3010 return.

How to file

Through MyBA

To file a return online, sign into MyBA and then select "File a return".

When you file your T3010 return online, MyBA identifies all necessary forms, sections, and schedules you need to complete, and reminds you to attach required documentation.

Since the forms are interactive, MyBA will only generate relevant schedules and appendices based on your previously selected answers.

MyBA helps to ensure you have submitted a complete T3010 return based on your charity's situation.

By mail

If you are not able to file a complete T3010 return online, you can contact the Charities Directorate to requests a paper form.

When you file a paper T3010 return, you must include all of the following forms and documents:

  • Form T3010, Registered Charity Information Return (the most updated version)
  • Form T1235, Directors/Trustees and Like Officials Worksheet
  • Form T1236, Qualified donees worksheet / Amounts provided to other organizations (if applicable)
  • Form T2081, Excess corporate holding worksheet for private foundations (if applicable)
  • Financial statements (even if your charity was inactive)

When filing your T3010 return, make sure your public contact information is still valid. This allows the public and the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) to communicate with your charity.

Mail the completed return and all required documentation to:

Charities Directorate
Canada Revenue Agency
105 – 275 Pope Road
Summerside PE C1N 6E8

When your return is mailed, the postmark date indicated on the envelope is used as the received date.

If you mail your complete T3010 return to an address other than the Charities Directorate (such as a tax services office), processing will be delayed.

For more information on how to complete your T3010 return, refer to Guide T4033, Completing Form T3010 Registered Charity Information Return.

If your charity is incorporated in Ontario

If your charity is incorporated in Ontario and subject to the Ontario Not-for-profit Corporations Act, you must file an Ontario annual information return. For more information, visit Ontario Business Registry.

If you fail to submit your return

If you do not file your return within 6 months of the end of your fiscal year we will start the revocation process for failure to file.

We will send you a T2051A, Notice of Intention to Revoke a Charity's Registration, by registered mail. You have 45 days from the date of this notice to file your return and 90 days to file an objection.
