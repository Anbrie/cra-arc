Calculate EI deductions

How to use the EI premiums tables and how to calculate manually the amount to withhold.

On this page

  • Calculation methods
  • References

Calculation methods

Reminder: There are online calculators that will calculate the EI deductions for you.

Calculate EI deductions based on the type of payment:

  • Regular payments (EI tables)
  • Any payments (manual calculation)
  • Year-end verification or multiple pay periods
Regular payments (EI tables)

EI premiums tables calculate the required EI premiums for you on given ranges of income for a specified pay period.

Steps

  1. Determine if you can use EI premiums tables

    EI premiums tables can be used in most situations.

    When to use or not to use the tax tables

    Use when

    • The payment is for regular employment income or was made with regular pay for one of the common or uncommon pay periods for which tables are provided
    • If you received Form TD1-IN (in addition to the TD1) from your employee, only use these tables on the portion of their income which is taxable

    Do not use when

    • The payment (for example, bonuses, commissions, retroactive pay) is made separately from regular pay
    • Your pay period does not have a table provided in the common or uncommon pay periods per year
    • You are doing a year-end verification of your EI premiums
    • The employee already reached their EI premium contributions for the year

  2. Determine if the employee reached the maximum contribution

    • Maximum annual employee EI premiums  in their employment with you
    • minus Employee's contributions to date for the year in their employment with you
    • equals This amount is the maximum EI contribution that you can deduct from your employee's pay for the rest of the year
    Calculation example
    • Do not deduct EI contributions if they have reached the maximum.
    • Continue to next step if they have not reached the maximum.

  3. Get the EI premiums tables

    2022 tax year

    Use the EI premiums table that matches your employee's province of employment.

    • Outside Quebec: Option 1

      Employment insurance (EI) premiums tables [PDF]

    • Inside Quebec: Option 2

      Quebec employment insurance (EI) premiums tables [PDF]

    Previous years

    Previous years

    To view, refer to: Common pay periods (T4032)

  4. Determine the pay range

    Find the range that includes your employee's insurable earnings (including any taxable benefits) in the "Pay" column

  5. Get the amount of EI contributions to deduct

    Find the amount in the "EI" column which is next to the column "Pay"

  6. Calculate the amount of CPP contributions you have to withhold

    Use the amount that applies to your situation to calculate step 7:

    You have to remit this amount and the same amount for your share of the EI contribution.

    • If the amount in step 5 is less than step 2, withhold the amount from step 5
    • If the amount in step 5 is greater than step 2, withhold the amount from step 2

  7. Calculate the amount of EI contributions you have to remit

    • EI contributions you have to withhold (step 6)
    • multiply by 1.4 or your reduced EI rate 
    • equals This is the total amount you have to remit: your employee's share and your share of the EI contribution
    Calculation example
Any payments (manual calculation)

Manual calculation can also be used for all payments made to employees to calculate the EI contribution that must be withheld for the pay period.

Steps

  1. Determine if the employee reached the maximum contribution

  2. Calculate the total insurable income

    • Employee's gross pay for the period
    • plus Employee's taxable benefits and allowances for the period
    • minus Total earnings that do not require EI contributions
    • equals This is the total insurable income
    Calculation example

  3. Determine the amount of EI contributions to deduct

    • Total insurable income (step 3)
    • multiply by EI rate 
    • equals This amount is the EI contribution to deduct
    Calculation example

  4. Calculate the amount of EI contributions you have to withhold

    Use the amount that applies to your situation to calculate step 6:

    • If the amount in step 4 is less than step 1, withhold the amount from step 4
    • If the amount in step 4 is greater than step 1, withhold the amount from step 1

  5. Calculate the amount of EI contributions you have to remit

    • EI contributions you have to withhold (step 5)
    • multiply by 1.4 or your reduced EI rate 
    • equals This is the total amount you have to remit: your employee's share and your share of the EI contribution
    Calculation example
Year-end verification or multiple pay periods

Year-end or multiple pay period verification is used to determine if you have deducted properly, under deducted or over deducted EI contributions for the period.

Steps

  1. Get the amount of insurable earnings for the period of employment

    • Employee’s total salary, wages benefits, and allowances for the total period which will be included in box 14 of their T4 slip
    • minus Employee’s earnings from employment, benefits, and payments from which you do not deduct EI
    • equals This amount is the insurable earnings for the period of employment

  2. Calculate the amount of insurable earnings for the period of employment

    Use the amount that applies to your situation to calculate step 3:

    • If the amount in step 1 is less than Maximum annual insurable earnings  in their employment with you, use this amount
    • If the amount in step 1 is greater than Maximum annual insurable earnings  in their employment with you, use the maximum pensionable earnings

  3. Determine the amount of required EI contributions for the period

    • Insurable earnings for the period of employment (step 2)
    • minus Basic exemption amount  that matches your pay period multiply by Number of pay periods of pensionable earnings
    • equals Employee’s contributory earnings for the period
    • multiply by EI rate 
    • equals This amount is the employee's required EI contributions for the period

  4. Determine if you have under or over remitted for the period

    • Employee's required EI contributions for the period (step 3)
    • minus EI contributions you deducted from the employee for the period
    • equals This amount should be $0 if you have deducted correctly
    • If the amount is positive you have under deducted for the year
    • If the amount is negative you have over deducted for the year

    To correct a deduction error:

    • If you have not filed the information return, refer to: Make corrections before filing
    • If you already filed the information return, refer to: Make corrections after filing
