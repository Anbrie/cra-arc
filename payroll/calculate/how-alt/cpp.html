Understand what a CPP contribution table is

A CPP contribution table is [description here].

Note: The annual basic exemption is built into the CPP tables.

Use when

  • If the payment is made with regular pay
  • If you received Form TD1-IN (in addition to the TD1) from your employee, only use these tables on the portion of their income which is taxable with the claims information reported on the TD1

Do not use when

  • If the payment (for example, bonuses, commissions, retroactive pay or other types of regular payments) is made separately from regular pay
  • If the payment is for deductions in a single period, multiple periods, or year-end verification
  • If the employee reached the CPP or EI maximum contributions for the year
  • If you received Form TD1X from your employee
  • If you received Form TD3F from the individual, see Fishers and employment insurance

Calculate CPP on regular payments

Steps

  1. Determine if you can use CPP contribution tables

    CPP contribution tables can be used in most situations. Refer to: When to use CPP contribution tables 

  2. Determine if you need to stop deducting CPP

    For current employees or pensioners, determine if they have reached the employee reached the maximum contribution for the year .

    If they have reach the maximum, refer to: Example manual CPP calculation

    If they have not reached the maximum, proceed to the next step.

  3. Get the CPP contributions tables

    Use the CPP contributions tables   that matches your pay period.

  4. Determine the date range

    Find the range that includes your employee's gross remuneration in the "Pay" column, include any taxable benefits

  5. [verb-based heading]

    Find the amount in the "CPP" column which is next to the the column "Pay"

  6. [verb-based heading]

    This amount is the CPP contribution that you should withhold from your employee's pay.

    You have to remit this amount and the same amount for your share of the CPP contribution.
