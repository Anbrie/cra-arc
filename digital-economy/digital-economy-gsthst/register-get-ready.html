Get ready to register for the GST/HST

You will be able to register on June 21, 2021.

The new rules for non-resident digital economy businesses start on July 1, 2021.

This information is for non-resident digital economy businesses who need to register for the GST/HST. If you are a Canadian resident business and need to register for the GST/HST, see GST/HST for businesses.

What you need before you start

Simplified GST/HST

You will need all of the following information with you to register for a simplified GST/HST account:

  • Effective date of registration

    If you register between June 21, 2021 and June 30, 2021, your registration date must be July 1, 2021 or later.

    After July 1, 2021, your effective date of registration will usually be the day you apply for your simplified GST/HST account.

  • Fiscal year for GST/HST purposes

    The fiscal year for the simplified GST/HST is December 31 of each year.

  • Fiscal year for GST/HST purposes Total annual revenue

    The total annual revenue information you provide on your registration form must be in Canadian dollars (CAD).

    To calculate your total annual revenue, include revenues from:

    • your taxable sales and other supplies
    • taxable supplies of all your associates

    Do not include revenues from:

    • exempt supplies
    • financial services
    • sales of capital property
    • goodwill from the sale of the business

    New businesses: If you are just starting your business, you may give us a reasonable estimate of your income for the year.

  • Basic business and personal information

    Business information

    You must provide the following information about your business:

    • business name
    • BN (if the business already has one)
    • type of business or organization (such as sole proprietor, partnership, corporation, registered charity)
    • physical address
    • mailing address (if different from the physical address)
    • address where the books and records of the business are maintained
    • e-mail address for the business
    • web site address, if applicable
    • description of major business activity

    Personal information

    You must provide the following information about the business owner(s):

    • names, title and phone #
    • Social Insurance Number (SIN) – if applicable
    • Temporary Tax Number (TTN) – if applicable
  • Supporting documents

    In addition to completing the registration web form, you may be required to provide documentation with supporting information to complete your registration.

    Examples of supporting information include:

    • proof of business registration in your country of residence, such as a confirmation of registration letter from the country’s tax authority
    • confirmation of inclusion in an incorporation register)

Calculating your net tax in a qualifying foreign currency

If you are a non-resident business and registering under the simplified GST/HST regime, you can request to calculate your net tax on your return and remit your payment in a qualifying foreign currency.

The approved qualifying foreign currency types are the United States dollar (USD) and the euro (EUR).

Normal GST/HST

You will need all of the following information with you to register for a normal GST/HST account:

  • Effective date of registration

    As a registrant under the normal GST/HST regime, your effective date of registration is no later than:

    • the day of the supply that made you exceed $30,000, if you exceed the $30,000 threshold in a single calendar quarter
    • the beginning of the month after you are no longer a small supplier, if you exceed the $30,000 threshold over the previous four (or fewer) consecutive calendar quarters (but not in a single calendar quarter)

    If you have not exceeded the $30,000 threshold over the previous four (or fewer) consecutive calendar quarters, your effective date of registration is usually the day you request your GST/HST account (or up to 30 days before that day).

    Fiscal year for GST/HST purposes

    Usually, your fiscal year for GST/HST purposes is the same as your tax year for income tax purposes. Generally, the tax year of the following persons is a calendar year:

    • individuals and certain trusts
    • professional corporations that are members of a partnership (such as a corporation that is the professional practice of an accountant, a lawyer, or a doctor)
    • partnerships, where at least one member of the partnership is an individual, a professional corporation or another affected partnership

    However, some persons use non-calendar tax years. If you are a person described above that uses a non-calendar tax year approved by the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA), you may want to use that same year as your GST/HST fiscal year.

    A corporation generally uses the same fiscal year for both income tax purposes and GST/HST purposes. However, if a corporation has a non-calendar tax year for income tax purposes, it can elect to use a calendar year for its GST/HST fiscal year.

    If you are a corporation that uses a non-calendar year for both income tax purposes and GST/HST purposes, and you change to another non-calendar tax year for income tax purposes, inform the CRA of the change as soon as possible and your GST/HST fiscal year will be changed to match it. For more information, see Open or manage an account – Make changes: Fiscal year.

  • Total annual revenue

    To calculate your total annual revenue, include revenues from:

    • your taxable sales, leases, and other supplies, including supplies that are zero-rated
    • taxable supplies of all your associates
    • fiscal year end

    Do not include revenues from:

    • exempt supplies
    • financial services
    • sales of capital property
    • goodwill from the sale of the business

    The total annual revenue information you provide on your registration form must be in Canadian dollars (CAD).
    New businesses: If you are just starting your business, you may give us a reasonable estimate of your income for the year.

  • Personal information

    You also must provide all of the following information about the business owner(s):

    • names, title and phone #
    • Social Insurance Number (SIN) – if applicable
    • Temporary Tax Number (TTN) – if applicable
  • Basic business information

    You must provide the following information about your business:

    • business name
    • BN (if the business already has one)
    • type of business or organization (such as sole proprietor, partnership, corporation, registered charity)
    • physical address
    • mailing address (if different from the physical address)
    • address where the books and records of the business are maintained
    • e-mail address for the business
    • web site address, if applicable
    • description of major business activity

    • Supporting documents

    In addition to completing the registration web form, you may be required to provide documentation with supporting information to complete your registration.

    An example of supporting information could be proof of business registration in your country of residence (such as a confirmation of registration letter from the country’s tax authority or confirmation of inclusion in an incorporation register).

    Calculating and providing security for your GST/HST account

    If you estimate that you need to post security with us, you will be expected to provide it as soon as possible after your registration is complete. You don’t have to provide either the security or an exemption form to complete your registration.

    For more information on calculating security, see is available in the guide, Doing Business in Canada, GST/HST information for Non-Residents.

    Registering for other CRA program accounts

    When you are registering under the normal GST/HST regime, you can also register your business for other CRA program accounts at the same time.

    For more information on the registration requirements for the most common CRA program accounts a business may need, see:

    • Payroll deductions (RP), if your business pays employees
    • Corporation income tax (RC), if your business is incorporated
    • Import-export (RM), if your business imports goods or sells goods or services abroad

    A small number of businesses will also need an account for specialized programs such as the Air travellers security charge. See Other CRA program accounts for more information.

    Registering for a GST/HST account will get you a business number if you don't already have one. If you already have a business number, the program account will be added to your business number.

    Your business will only ever have one business number

    Who can register your business

    Business owners, including partners, directors, and officers, can register the business.
    A person other than the business owner, such as an accountant, lawyer or another company employee, can also submit the registration form on behalf of your business.

    Authorized representatives

    An authorized representative can request information about your registered GST/HST account. They may also be able to change certain account information, make payment arrangements or communicate with the CRA on your behalf.

    To become an authorized representative of the business, you or the business owner must:

    • register your business and have a business number
    • submit an AUT-01 Form to grant you authorization

    If you are identified as a contact for the registration of the business, this means we may contact you to request or confirm information related to the registration request.

    After you submit your registration form

    Reference number

    You will receive a reference number after you submit the online registration form.

    You can use this reference number to ask about the status of your registration. You many be contacted by the CRA by phone to collect or validate information.

    Business number

    You will receive a business number once we review and validate the information you provided. We may contact you by phone or send you a letter with your business number.

    You will also be emailed a registration package. This package includes general information about registration, additional forms you may need. We will also send you an e-mail with links to tax guides and general tax information.

    If you have questions

    For questions or more information about non-resident GST/HST registration or security, you can contact us.
