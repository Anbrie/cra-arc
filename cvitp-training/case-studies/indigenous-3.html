Takeaway points

Steps to follow

  1. Review their background information and the required slips
  2. In the Interview setup, check the boxes next to:
    • Tax return for a Status Indian in the Specific situations section and Employment income and employment insurance benefits (T4, T4E/RL-6) in the Employment and other benefits section
    • Pension income, other income and split pension income, COVID-19 benefits (T4A, T4A(OAS), T4A(P), T4A-RCA, T4RSP, T4RIF, T1032) in the Pension section
    • Social assistance, worker’s compensation (T5007/RL-5) in the Employment and other benefits section
  3. Click Status Indian in the left side menu:
    • select Yes at the field, Please confirm that you are a registered Indian, or entitled to be registered as an Indian under the Indian Act
    • enter $5,000 in the field for Exempt benefits paid due to the COVID-19
  4. Click T4 and pension income in the left side menu and click the + sign next to T4 income (earned in any province except Quebec), then complete the necessary fields:
    • select Exempt CPP/QPP (rare!) for field 28
    • Box 71 – Indian (exempt income) – Employment can be found in the drop-down menu under Other information
  5. Click T4A and pension income in the left side menu and click the + sign next to T4A – Pension, retirement, annuity, and other income (COVID-19 benefits)
    • Field 202 can be found in the drop-down menu under Other information


Heather’s CRB income is tax exempt because the income that entitled her to the CRB was tax exempt under the Indian Act. The software will automatically generate form T90 which shows her tax exempt income and allows Heather to include this income in the calculation of the Canada workers benefit (CWB).
