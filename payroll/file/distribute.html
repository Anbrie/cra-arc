When to distribute

Generally, if you are distributing:

  • T4, T4A, T4A-NR and T4A-RCA slips, you must provide your employees with their slips by the last day of February following the calendar year to which the slips apply
  • T1204 or T5018 slips, you are not required to distribute the slips (you may still provide a copy of the slip to the recipient)

Which method to use

You can distribute slips using any of the following methods:

By email

If you are distributing slips by email, you must receive written consent from employees before sending the slips by email.

Employer's electronic portal

If you are distributing slips using an employer's electronic portal, you must:

  • Make the slips accessible to your employees on a secure portal and with a secure printer.
  • Provide your employee the option for paper copies.
By paper

If you are distributing slips by paper:

  • You must provide your employee with two copies of the T4 slip in person or by mail.
  • We suggests that the two copies be generated on one sheet.
  • For security purposes, your payroll program account number (box 54) should not be printed on these copies.
  • Once a slip is completed, you may distribute photocopies to their employees.

What happens if you distribute late or you do not distribute

If you distribute the slips late or you do not distribute, you may be assessed a penalty of $25 daily per slip with a minimum penalty of $100 and a maximum of $2,500.

What happens if you cannot distribute

If the slips are returned as undeliverable, you should keep the copies with the employee's file.

If an employee's address is known to be incorrect, the slips must not be sent at that address. You must:

  • Document why the copies were not sent and the actions taken to obtain the correct address
  • Keep this information with the slip copies in the employee's file
  • Include the slip in the information return when filing

What records to keep

You must keep your books and records in case we ask to see them later.

Learn more: Keeping records
