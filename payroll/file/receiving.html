Receiving payroll correspondence from us

Once we receive your information returns, we will send you a notice of assessment if either:

  • the CRA owes you a refund or rebate
  • your amount owing is more than the payment you made

We may also send you other type of correspondence depending on the situation.

Notice to file (TX14PD)

What is a TX14PD notice

The TX14PD notice is sent to you if you have an active payroll account and you have not filed your T4 type information returns (T4, T4A, T4A-NR, T4E, T4RIF, T4RSP). They are prepared the third Friday in May and mailed out the following business day.

What to do if you received a TX14PD notice

If you are required to file T4 type information returns, you must file within 30 days of the date on TX14PD notice to avoid additional penalties or legal action.

What if you received a TX14PD notice by error

If you were not required to file T4 type information returns and you received the TX14PD by error, send us a response as per the instructions on the notice.

What is a PIER review

Every year, the CRA reviews the information on the T4 slips to ensure that the following figures match:

  • Required amounts of CPP and EI calculated from the employee's pensionable and insurable earnings
  • Reported amounts of CPP and EI on the employee's T4 slips

How are the results provided

If there are discrepancies between the required and reported amounts, the results are printed on a PIER listing. If you report electronically and report an employee number on the T4 slip, you can view the employee number on the PIER listing.

The report includes:

  • PIER listing which includes the affected employees and the figures used by CRA in the calculations
  • PIER summary that shows any balance due
Examples of common errors
  • Box 28 (Exempt CPP/QPP, EI, or PPIP) of the T4 slip is not completed correctly
  • An employee's change of age has resulted in a change in pensionable status (turned 18 or 70)
  • The SIN and/or name on the T4 was entered incorrectly
  • An employee has exceeded the maximum rate of CPP and/or EI contributions but you did not stop the deductions

What is the timeline for a PIER process

Timeline Steps in the PIER process
Day 1 Initial letter (PIER package) sent to you which requests a reply within 30 calendar days
Day 45 If you do not reply or make a payment in full, an automatic assessment is raised and a notice of assessment is issued.
Day 65 We will issue amended T4s to you.

What methods we used to send you the PIER notification

The PIER will be sent to you in one of the following methods:

  • Electronically when you are registered for MyBA or RAC in the PIER overview option
    • PIER information will be available in MyBA and RAC for the last 5 years
    • You can view, download, and reply to the PIER letter in MyBA or RAC
  • Mail if you are not registered for the electronic services

How to respond to a PIER

You are not required to respond to the PIER if you agree with our calculations and you will be remitting (paying) the exact amount shown on the PIER.

What if you disagree with the PIER

You are required to respond to the PIER if you do not agree with our calculations. You must respond by returning the PIER with any corrected information and an explanation. The response can be sent using:

  • MyBA or RAC
  • Fax number or the address provided on the PIER

If you do not reply or make a payment in full by the reply date noted on the PIER, an automatic assessment is raised and a notice of assessment (NOA) is issued to you. The NOA will include applicable penalties or interest, or both.

In either situation, do not send amended T4 slips. We will issue any amended slip to you.

What are the payment methods

If you are using MyBA, you will have a Proceed to pay option in the PIER Overview section. This will allow you to do either of the following:

  • Pay the PIER deficiencies in full without replying
  • Pay any remaining deficiencies after you provided a response to their PIER

This payment option is not available in RAC.

What if adjustments are required on T4 slips

If adjustments are required on T4 slips due to a PIER review, we will prepare two copies of the amended T4 slip and forward them to you. One copy for your records and one copy to be sent to your employee.

Discrepancy notice (PD4R)

What is a PD4R notice

A PD4R notice is issued to you when there is a difference between the total remittances made in the year and the total of the reported amounts on the T4 type slips, such as the T4 and T4A. If you:

  • remitted more than the amounts reported on the slips, you will have a credit balance on your payroll account. A credit balance is not automatically transferred to the current year or refunded to you because you may have made an error on the T4 type slips that requires amending.
  • remitted less than the amounts reported on the slips, you will have a debit balance in your payroll account. A Balance due remittance voucher is included with the PD4R notice in this situation.

When is a PD4R issued

Any of the following issues may result in a PD4R being issued:

Remittance errors

Remittance errors can occur when:

  • there are missing remittances or extra remittances
  • incorrect amounts are remitted

If you remitted more than the required amount for the year, a refund or a transfer can be requested when responding to the notice. We will review the account to determine if you are entitled to the credit. If you are entitled, the transaction will be shown on the your next statement.

If you remitted less than the required amount for the year, you must make a payment as soon as possible. Penalties and interest may apply.

Learn more: How to remit (pay)

Information return errors

Information return errors can occur when:

  • there are missing slips or extra slips
  • incorrect amounts are reported

You must correct a discrepancy caused by an information return error by submitting amended slips. You can include the amended slips when responding to the PD4R.

Learn more: Amend, cancel, add, or replace slips and returns

PD27 - Temporary Wage Subsidy Self-Identification Form

The following situations may be related to a PD4R notice. However, a discrepancy resulting from a TWS claim may not be only limited to these situations.

  • Your used the TWS but never completed Form PD27.
  • TWS request ongoing due to missing information on a previously submitted Form PD27.
  • Filed Form PD27 has not been processed yet.
  • Claimed amount is not included in Line 82 and difference on T4 Summary paid. You must reply to the PD4R notice and request the credit balance to be either refunded or transferred to the current year.

Learn more: 10% Temporary Wage Subsidy for Employers

How to respond to a PD4R notice

You must respond to the PD4R notice and provide an explanation, even if a request has been made to correct the discrepancy.

What happens if you do not respond

If you do not respond to the PD4R notice, we will issue a PD7D - Notice of assessment when there is a debit balance. Once a PD7D has been issued, you must follow the instructions on the PD7D. The response can be sent using:

  • MyBA or RAC
  • Fax number or the address provided on the PD4R (using the Yearly payroll breakdown form)
Notice of assessment (PD7D)

What is a PD7D notice

The PD7D is sent when amounts are assessed on your payroll deduction account.

The PD7D consists of both:

  • the notice of assessment
  • a remittance voucher that is used to pay an existing balance owing

It is not a statement of account.

The PD7D cannot be used to remit current payroll remittances. You must use the PD7A to remit (pay).

Learn more: Remit (pay) payroll deductions contributions

How to respond to a PD7D notice

The method to respond to a PD7D depends on the reason for the assessment.

Assessment related to a PIER report

You must review the original PIER report.

Learn more: Receiving a PIER

Assessment not related to a PIER report

You are not required to respond to the PD7D if you agree with our calculations and you will be remitting (paying) the exact amount shown on the notice of assessment.

What if you disagree with the PD7D notice of assessment

If you disagree, you may want to register a formal dispute.
