Calculations using the EI premiums tables

Information on how to calculate and to determine the 2022 payroll deductions for amounts you pay your employees or pensioners in different pay periods.

We encourage you to take advantage of our electronic payroll deductions services. Use PDOC to quickly calculate payroll deductions for all federal, provinces and territories except Quebec. PDOC calculates payroll deductions for the most common pay periods (such as weekly or biweekly), based on exact salary figures.

Determine the EI premiums to withhold

You need to get the specific tax tables based on your determination of the province of employment, learn more: Determine the province of employment (POE)

Learn more: Learn about EI, the premium rates and maximums

  1. For current employees or pensioners, determine if you need to stop deducting EI because the employee reached the maximum premium for the year 

    Do not use the tax tables if they reached the maximum annual premium. To calculate, go to Manual calculations of EI premiums deductions.

  2. Get the EI premiums tables   that matches your pay period.

  3. Find the range that includes your employee's gross remuneration in the "Insurable earnings" column, include any taxable benefits

  4. Find the amount in the "Insurable premium" column which is next to the column "Insurable earnings"

  5. This amount is the EI premium that you should withhold from your employee's pay.

    You have to remit this amount and the same amount for your share of the EI premium.
