Do not use inserted text

This feature mimics the visual appearance of a link, which causes usability problems.

Correct use

Bold text
  • Use bold for emphasis, but use it sparingly: the more you use it, the less effective it is
Italicized text
  • People with dyslexia or other reading disorders may find it difficult to read italicized text
  • Don't use italics:
    • for design or decorative purposes
    • to emphasize a word or phrase (use bold sparingly instead)
    • for long passages of text, such as quotations
    • in page titles
Marked text
  • Use to highlight a section of text for reference purposes, due to its relevance in another context
  • Highlight text to show matching search terms or to show text in relation to something else
  • Do not use in place of bold (<strong>) text
    • This tag is not meant to simply highlight key words, phrases, numbers or amount totals
Deleted text
  • Use to show recently deleted text
  • Use sparingly, as it makes a page difficult to read
  • Use to show record changes to an official document, such as meeting minutes or a law
  • Use as a temporary effect on a page
Strikethrough text
  • Use to indicate text that is no longer accurate or no longer relevant
  • Use sparingly, as it makes a page difficult to read
  • Use to show record changes to an official document, such as meeting minutes or a law
  • Use as a temporary effect on a page
Inserted text
  • Do not use this feature
    • It mimics the visual appearance of a link, which causes usability problems
    • Non-links should not look like links
Underlined text
  • Do not use this feature
    • It mimics the visual appearance of a link, which causes usability problems
    • Non-links should not look like links
