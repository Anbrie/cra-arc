Guidelines

A Community Volunteer Income Tax Program (CVITP) volunteer can take on many different roles. The following guidelines will provide you with suggestions and tips to help you succeed whether you prepare tax returns or provide support.

To ensure the program's success, organizations and volunteers must safeguard taxpayer information and understand their responsibilities in this respect.

  • Do not use or disclose the individual's information for any purpose other than to complete their return
  • Store the individual's information securely in a locked drawer or cabinet and never leave their information unattended
  • Be mindful of your surroundings when speaking with individuals, especially in a virtual clinic setting when other people are present in your home or you have technological devices such as Google Home or Alexa
  • In the event of any type of security breach, such as lost, stolen or compromised data, tell your community organization and your Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) coordinator immediately

Screening for eligibility

You must screen the individual’s information to ensure they meet the eligibility criteria for the CVITP  . For more information, refer to the eligibility criteria under Introduction to free tax clinics.

File my Return

This CRA service offers eligible individuals the ability to file their tax return by answering a series of short questions through an automated phone service. Only individuals who receive an invitation letter from the CRA can participate.

If during the screening process an individual confirms they received a File my Return invitation letter, volunteers should verify if they contacted the CRA.

  • If they have not yet contacted the CRA, volunteers may suggest they do so, or offer to complete their tax return in person or virtually by using the provided tax preparation software
  • If the individual contacted the CRA to use File my Return, no further action is required on the volunteer’s behalf

For more information on this automated service, refer to File my Return.

Confirming the individual's identity

You must get proof of identity from the individual before you prepare their return.

Proof of identity must:

  • be valid
  • be government issued
  • include a photo

Examples of valid identification include:

  • provincial or territorial (or equivalent abroad) driver's license or photo identification
  • Canadian or foreign passport
  • Indian status and identification card
  • military or other government employee identification card

How to confirm the individual’s identity in virtual clinics using one of the options:

  • The organization may have already confirmed an individual’s identity and you will be advised if this is the case. You may also already know the individual personally and can confirm their identity. If so, you do not need to authenticate further.
  • Using a video-conferencing application, you can visually confirm the individual’s identification on camera. Ask the individual to display their photo ID for verification.
  • You may use Auto-fill my return (AFR) to confirm the individual’s identity. You will need to review the information in Before you begin and how to use AFR . Once the AFR process is completed confirm their identity using the information exported.
    • For example, without divulging any information, you may ask the individual to confirm their employer’s name, their source of income or the amount showing in a specific box on one of their slips.
  • If you are unable to use options 1, 2 or 3, you may contact the CRA by email and request that they confirm the individual’s identity over the phone.

    How to email the CRA
    Send an email to VIRTUALG@cra-arc.gc.ca with “Taxpayer’s authentication” in the subject line.  In the body of the email, include the following information:

    • Date and time the volunteer spoke to the individual - with the time zone details
    • Volunteer’s full name, province and the first three digits of their EFILE number, if applicable
    • Individual’s name, contact information and language of correspondence
    • Call back date and time requested

You can include the names of up to five individuals in each email to the CRA.

The CRA will make every effort to call the individual at the date and time requested, however this may not always be possible.

Advise the individual:

  • to expect a call within one (1) business day or on the requested date and time
  • to expect the CRA agent to confirm the date the individual and the volunteer determined (this will ensure the individual is not dealing with a fraudster)
  • to be ready to respond to the CRA employee’s questions to confirm their identity by having information such as prior year tax returns, T4 slips, etc. available  

The CRA will advise you, the volunteer, by email of the outcome of the call. If the individual’s identity was confirmed, you can contact the individual and proceed with completing their return. If not, follow the instructions provided in the email.

The CRA will try to offer assistance in the preferred language of the individual, however this may not always be possible. Usually, if a preferred language request is made, the turnaround time on the call may be longer than one (1) business day.

If you are assisting the individual’s legal representative or executor, it is important to validate their identity and ensure they show proof of the Power of attorney (POA)they have for the individual for whom you are preparing a tax return. If they do not have the legal documentation on hand, you can:

  • contact the dedicated help line for volunteers
  • have the individual speak with the CRA telephone agent to answer confidentiality questions
  • confirm if the CRA has a record of the authorization on file

Signing the authorization forms

TIS60, Community Volunteer Income Tax Program – Taxpayer Authorization

Before you start preparing a tax return for an individual, they must complete and sign Section I – Authorization of form TIS60, Community Volunteer Income Tax Program – Taxpayer Authorization. This authorizes you to prepare their tax return as a CVITP volunteer.

A TIS60 must be signed for each tax return you prepare.

  • Part A – Identification identifies the individual
  • Part B – Disclaimer, by signing this form the individual acknowledges that their tax return is being prepared by a CVITP volunteer. The individual whose information is in Part A must sign Part B. It may also be signed by a legal representative on the individual’s behalf, provided they have the authorization to do so.

If you use the Auto-fill my return service to prepare the tax return, the individual must also sign the Authorization request – signature page. For more information, refer to Auto-fill my return.

Signing form TIS60 in virtual clinics

If you are participating in a free virtual clinic, you can get an individual’s verbal consent in lieu of signing the authorizations, by video conference, telephone, or the method pre-arranged by your organization. However, individuals must be aware and agree to the following conditions:

  1. You are a volunteer under the CVITP and not acting as an agent of the Canada Revenue Agency
    Read the script to the individual:

    Hello, my name is (insert name). I’m a volunteer from the Community Volunteer Income Tax Program. I will prepare your income tax and benefits return(s) electronically for you with the information you supplied.

    I am a volunteer with (organization name) here to provide this service to you. I am not an employee of the Canada Revenue Agency.

    Do I have your permission to complete and file your income tax return?

  2. The method(s) of communication may potentially be unsecure
    Read the script to the individual:

    We will be discussing your personal information so that I may prepare your tax return. I have and will continue to take all reasonable efforts to ensure information is kept safe, however, the method of communication we are using may not be secure.

    Regarding use of cell phone, video or email:

    Do you consent to the use of a cell phone or cordless telephone to discuss information needed to prepare your income tax and benefits return(s)?

    Do you consent to the use of videoconference (insert name of application) to discuss information needed to prepare your income tax and benefits return(s)?

    Do you consent to the use of unsecure email to exchange written discussions, communication and documents needed to prepare your income tax and benefits return(s)?

  3. Service will not be provided if the individual chooses to discontinue the call
    Read the script to the individual:

    You may choose to discontinue this call at any time. If you choose to do so, or fail to provide the information needed to prepare your tax return, I will not be able to assist you.

    Do you acknowledge what I just read to you?

Once you receive verbal consent, Section I – Authorization of the TIS60 is considered completed.

Gathering and verifying the individual’s information

Organizing the slips and receipts as they apply to the different sections of the Income Tax Return may help you create an efficient working method. For example, keeping all T4 slips together so they are easy to find when entering employment income information in the tax software, can save you a lot of time.

The most common information slips and receipts may include:

  • T4, T5, T5007, T4AA and T4(OAS)
  • receipts for child care, medical expenses, and RRSP contributions
  • charitable donation receipts, including those made in the current year and any donations made and not claimed within the last 5 years
  • tuition receipts, such as T2202, TL11A, or TL11B

Confirm with the individual that they verified the accuracy of the information reported on the slips (for example, name, SIN, and issuer). If a slip has inaccurate information, the individual must contact the issuer of the slip to have it corrected. Individuals waiting for slip issue to be resolved, are still encouraged to file by the deadline. This will ensure there are no interruptions to any benefit or credit payments they may be entitled to, and helps avoiding late-filing penalties.

To make sure you have all the information needed to prepare a tax return, ask the individual you are assisting the following questions:

  • How many tax years do you need me to prepare for you? For what years?
  • Do you have any family members, such as a spouse or common-law partner, who must also file their tax return?
  • Do you have children or other dependants? How many? What are their ages?
  • Have you, your spouse or dependant(s) (if applicable) been approved for the T2201, Disability Tax Credit Certificate?
  • How many sources of income did you have this year? For example, multiple employers and interest income.
  • Has you living situation changed within the last year? This may include a change in marital status, address, or dependants (custody change, adoption, additional dependants)?
  • Did you receive or earn any tips?

Keep in mind, questions you need to ask may not be limited to this list!

How to obtain information if an individual is missing a slip

  • use Auto-fill my return
  • call the dedicated help line for volunteers at 1-866-398-3488
  • advise the individual to contact their employer or payer
  • use pay stubs or statements to estimate the missing income and deductions

The individual must be present if you call the dedicated help line for volunteers to obtain their information. The individual will need to answer confidentiality questions. If confidentiality is met, the individual can provide the agent with verbal authorization for you to speak on their behalf. Verbal authorization is only valid for the duration of that telephone call.

If you know or suspect that the information an individual provides to you is not accurate or contains omissions, withdraw your services.

Entering and verifying your user information

Before you start preparing tax returns, make sure you always enter your user information in the UFile CVITP, EFILE-NetFile setup. You will need the following information on hand:

  • your EFILE number and password
  • information about your organization:
    • name
    • city
    • CVITP Organization Identification Number (COIN), if provided

If you’re sharing a computer with other volunteers, verify the setup at the start of every shift to ensure your information is used when you are preparing tax returns.

For more related information and instructions, refer to EFILE-NetFile setup.
