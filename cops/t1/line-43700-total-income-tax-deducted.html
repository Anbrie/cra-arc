Line 43700 – Total income tax deducted

Note: Line 43700 was line 437 before tax year 2019.

Claim the total of the amounts shown as income tax deducted from all of your Canadian information slips.

Income tax deducted at source will be shown on the following information slips:

Box 22 

  • T4 slip, Statement of Remuneration Paid
  • T4A slip, Statement of Pension, Retirement, Annuity, and Other Income
  • T4A(OAS) slip, Statement of Old Age Security
  • T4A(P) slip, Statement of Canada Pension Plan Benefits
  • T4A-RCA slip, Statement of Distributions from a Retirement Compensation Arrangement (RCA)
  • T4E slip, Statement of Employment Insurance and Other Benefits

Box 28 

  • T4RIF slip, Statement of Income from a Registered Retirement Income Fund

Box 30 

  • T4RSP slip, Statement of RRSP Income

Completing your tax return

Follow the instructions below that apply to you.

Residents of a province other than Quebec on December 31, 2021

Claim the total of the amounts shown as income tax deducted from all of your Canadian information slips.

If you are electing to split your eligible pension income with your spouse or common-law partner, enter the result for income tax deducted from Form T1032, Joint Election to Split Pension Income, on line 43700 of your return. 

If you had Quebec provincial income tax withheld from your income, include those amounts on line 43700 of your return.

Residents of Quebec on December 31, 2021

Claim the total of the amounts shown as income tax deducted from all of your Canadian information slips.

If you are electing to split your eligible pension income with your spouse or common-law partner, enter the result for income tax deducted from Form T1032, Joint Election to Split Pension Income, on line 43700 of your return. 

Do not include any Quebec provincial income tax deducted on your federal return. Instead, claim this amount on your Revenu Québec Income Tax Return.

Notes

If you paid tax by instalments in 2021, claim the total of your instalments on line 47600 of your return.

If you paid foreign taxes, do not claim these amounts on line 43700 of your return. Instead, you may be able to claim a foreign tax credit. See line 40500.
