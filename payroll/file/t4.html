T4 slips and summary - Information for employers

On this page

T4 slip

When is income reported on a T4 slip

Income is reported on a T4 slip for the year in which it is paid, regardless of when it was earned.

What if your employee worked in more than one province

If your employee worked in more than one province or territory during the year, complete a T4 slip for each province or territory.

How to report the income paid in special situations

You may need to report the income paid differently in special situations. Before you complete the T4 slip, make sure to review the following special situations:

  • Amounts paid to self-employed barbers and hairdressers
  • Amounts paid to self-employed taxi drivers and drivers of other passenger-carrying vehicles
  • Amounts paid to fishers
  • Amounts paid to Indians
  • Amounts paid to placement or employment agency workers
  • Salary deferral arrangements
  • Retiring allowances
  • Employees with power saws or tree trimmers
  • Seasonal Agricultural Workers Program
  • Employees outside Canada
  • Salary overpayments

What are the guidelines for completing slips

  • If you have multiple payroll accounts, file a T4 return for each payroll account.
  • Do not show negative dollar amounts on slips. To make changes to previous years, see Make corrections after filing.
  • If a box does not have a value, do not enter "nil" or "N/A". Leave the box blank.
  • Do not change the headings of any of the boxes.
  • Report, in dollars and cents, all amounts you paid during the year, except pension adjustment amounts, which are reported in dollars only.
  • Report all amounts in Canadian dollars, even if they were paid in another currency. For more information, see
  • Do not enter hyphens or dashes between numbers.
  • Do not enter the dollar sign ($).

How to complete sections of the T4 slip

Common reporting pensionable and insurable earnings errors

Boxes 24, 26, and 28 are sometimes completed incorrectly.

  • Boxes 24 and 26 must always be completed even if there were no insurable or pensionable earnings.
  • Box 28 must have an X or a check mark in the EI box if there was no requirement to withhold EI premiums for the entire reporting period.
  • Box 28 must have an X or a check mark in the CPP box if there was no requirement to withhold CPP contributions for the entire reporting period.

How to complete the T4 slip boxes, including the list of codes?

Other information

The Other information area at the bottom of the T4 slip has boxes for you to enter codes and amounts that relate to:

  • employment commissions
  • taxable allowances and benefits
  • deductible amounts
  • fishers' income
  • tax-exempt income
  • retiring allowances
  • other entries

The boxes are not pre-numbered. Enter the codes and amounts that apply to the employee.

If more than six codes apply to the same employee (overflow T4 slips)

If you have more than six Other information codes to enter, use an additional T4 slip, but do not repeat all the data on the additional slip. Enter only:

  • employer name and address
  • employee SIN and name,
  • province code
  • applicable CPP/QPP and/or EI exempt status
  • pensionable and insurable earnings of zero
  • the required boxes in the Other information area (report each code, and amount only once)

T4 summary

What to do if you received the Temporary Wage Subsidy (TWS)

If you received the Temporary Wage Subsidy (TWS), you must include the 10% TWS (that was claimed on the PD27) on line 82 (remittances paid) of the T4 summary.  If the TWS amount is not included in the summary, it may generate a PD4R.

What are the guidelines for completing a summary

  • Report all amounts in Canadian dollars, even if they were paid in another currency. For more information, see
  • Complete a separate T4 Summary for each payroll account. The totals reported on a T4 summary must match the totals reports on the corresponding T4 slips. Errors or omissions can cause processing delays.
  • If there are no amounts to report on a T4 slip or summary, there is no need to send the return.
