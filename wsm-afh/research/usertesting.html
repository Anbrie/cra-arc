What is User Experience (UX)?

UX is every aspect of a client's interaction with the CRA website and our products. Good UX means that we are meeting the client's exact needs, without any fuss or bother.

What is UX research and testing?

UX research and testing refers to evaluating your web products, services, or applications through a number of different testing activities with representative users to better understand who your users are, how they find and use information on the CRA website, what they do, why they do it, and where they are encountering problems.

For example, during a usability test participants will try to complete typical tasks while observers watch, listen, and take notes. The goal of the tests is to identify any user-issues. These tests tell you what is working, what isn't, and most importantly, whether or not the web product or service is meeting the user’s needs.

What are the benefits of UX research and testing?

UX research and testing is the best way to observe what people actually do and think as they interact with your web product or service. While analytics software can tell you where people go and what they click, UX research and testing tells you why users stay on your site or why they decide to leave. UX research and testing also identifies the reasons why a user is unable to complete a task.

Through UX research and testing, you will achieve the following:

  • Learn if participants are able to complete specified tasks successfully.
  • Identify how long it takes participants to complete specified tasks.
  • Find out if participants can easily meet their objectives using your web product or service.
  • Identify and prioritize the changes required to improve performance.
  • Analyze the performance to see if it meets your business objectives.
  • Identify and fix problems early on before they are coded, leading to cost-savings.

How do you access UX research and testing?

DDPD's Web Services team will help you determine the right kind of UX research and testing to meet your objectives, depending on where you are in the development cycle. It can take a few weeks to arrange for UX research and testing.

See all steps involved…

  • Step 1: Email us with details about your objectives (What are you trying to improve? What is the problem you are trying to fix?).
  • Step 2: Web Services contacts you to discuss your requirements and to help you develop a Statement of Work (SOW) and provide you with a cost estimate based on past projects.
  • Step 3: Web Services places an order in Synergy to initiate the bidding process.
  • Step 4: Contract is awarded to the vendor with the lowest bid.
  • Step 5: Web Services collaborates with you and the vendor throughout the testing phase.
  • Step 6: Final reports are provided to you and to Web Services. Report summaries are posted on InfoZone.
