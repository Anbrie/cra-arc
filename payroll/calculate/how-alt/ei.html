Understand what EI premiums tables are

EI premiums table are [description here].

Use when

  • The payment is made with regular pay
  • You received Form TD1-IN (in addition to the TD1) from your employee, only use these tables on the portion of their income which is taxable with the claims information reported on the TD1

Do not use when

  • The payment (for example, bonuses, commissions, retroactive pay or other types of regular payments) is made separately from regular pay
  • The payment is for deductions in a single period, multiple periods, or year-end verification
  • The employee reached the EI maximum contributions for the year
  • You received Form TD1X from your employee
  • You received Form TD3F from the individual, refer to: Fishers and employment insurance

The annual basic exemption  is built into the EI tables.

Calculate EI on regular payments

Reminder: There are automated solutions the will calculate the EI deductions for you.

Steps

  1. Determine if you can use EI premiums tables

    EI premiums tables can be used in most situations. Refer to: When to use EI premiums tables 

  2. Determine if you need to stop deducting EI

    For current employees or pensioners, determine if they have reached the maximum premium for the year .

    If they have reach the maximum, refer to: Manual EI calculation

    If they have not reached the maximum, proceed to the next step.

  3. Get the EI premiums tables

    Use the EI premiums tables  that matches your pay period.

  4. Determine the date range

    Find the range that includes your employee's gross remuneration in the "Pay" column, include any taxable benefits

  5. [verb-based heading]

    Find the amount in the "EI" column which is next to the the column "Pay"

  6. [verb-based heading]

    This amount is the EI contribution that you should withhold from your employee's pay.

    You have to remit this amount and the same amount for your share of the EI contribution.

Calculate EI on bonuses, retroactive payments or irregular amounts

In addition to the regular types of payments, you must deduct EI premiums from bonuses, retroactive payments or other types of irregular payments.

When to use the bonus method

  • Use the bonus method

    If the payment is made separately from regular pay, use the bonus method to calculate manually

  • Do not use the bonus method

    If the payment is made with regular pay, use: Payroll Deductions Online Calculator (PDOC) or Calculations of CPP, EI and income tax deductions using the tax tables

Why use this bonus method

This method will result in a tax deduction that better estimates the amount of tax the individual will owe on the employment income when filing their personal income tax return. Using the bonus method provides a fairer tax deduction because the payment is spread over the year and taxed accordingly.

Steps

  1. Determine if you need to stop deducting EI

    For current employees or pensioners, determine if they have reached the maximum premium for the year .

    If they have reach the maximum, refer to: Manual EI calculation

    If they have not reached the maximum, proceed to the next step.
