Understand your tax obligations

If you earn money from any digital platform, you have obligations for income tax and the goods and services tax / harmonized sales tax (GST/HST).

Income tax obligations

As a resident of Canada, you are required to report your income from all sources.

Income tax obligations for self-employed

If you earn income through online channels, you may be considered to be self-employed and carrying on a business. Your tax obligations are different from those of an employee.

Identifying whether you are an employee or self-employed

Report and pay tax on all income earned from taxable activities

Income is net earnings from employment, self-employment (including most digital economy activities), investments or benefits.

Report and pay tax on all your self-employment income by completing line 2600 of your T1 income tax and benefit return and Form T2125, statement of Business or Professional Activities.

  • Line 2600 – Taxable income
  • T2125 Statement of Business or Professional Activities
  • How to fill in Form T2125, Form T2042, or Form T2121

Reporting all of your taxable income helps you by:

  • increasing your potential maximum amount for a mortgage or loan
  • increasing your contribution limits for registered retirement savings plans

GST/HST obligations

Generally, if you provide a good or service and earn more than $30,000 over 4 calendar quarters, then you must register for and collect the GST/HST.

  • When to register for and start charging the GST/HST
  • General information for GST/HST registrants

Ridesharing obligations

If you earn money from ridesharing (for example Uber or Lyft), then you must register for GST/HST regardless of how much you earn from ridesharing.

Canada Pension Plan (CPP) contributions

You will have to contribute to the Canada Pension Plan (CPP) if you:

  • are over the age of 18
  • earn more than $3,500 per year
  • work in Canada (other than the province of Quebec)

If you are self-employed, you are responsible to pay the employer and employee parts of your CPP contributions.

You do this using your income tax and benefit return’s Schedule 8: "CPP Contributions on Self-Employment and Other Earnings".

Correcting your tax affairs

When would you change a return compared to applying for voluntary correction?

If you did not report all income earned through the digital economy, you may have to pay penalties and interest. By correcting your tax affairs voluntarily, you can remove or reduce penalties and interest.

You can correct your tax affairs (including corrections to GST/HST returns) and report income that you did not report in previous years.

  • Income tax and benefit return changes
  • GST/HST return or rebate changes

Apply for a voluntary correction

The Voluntary Disclosures Program allows individuals to report income and pay any taxes owing before the CRA takes any action. To qualify, your application must:

  • be voluntary, which means you apply before you are aware of the CRA taking any compliance action against you or anyone related to you
  • be complete, which means that you have included all relevant information and documents (including all returns, forms and schedules needed to correct the error or omission)
  • include information that is at least one year beyond (for income tax) or one reporting period beyond (for GST/HST) the filing due date
  • include payment of the estimated tax owing
  • involve the application of a penalty (or interest for disclosures related to GST/HST)
