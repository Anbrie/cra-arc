Calculations of CPP, EI and income tax deductions using the tax tables

Information on how to calculate and to determine the 2022 payroll deductions for amounts you pay your employees or pensioners in different pay periods.

We encourage you to take advantage of our electronic payroll deductions services. Use PDOC to quickly calculate payroll deductions for all federal, provinces and territories except Quebec. PDOC calculates payroll deductions for the most common pay periods (such as weekly or biweekly), based on exact salary figures.

When to use the CPP, EI and income tax deductions tables

  • Use the tax tables

    If the payment is made with regular pay, use one of the following: the deductions tables, Payroll Deductions Online Calculator (PDOC) or Formulas for employers using software programs

    If you received Form TD1-IN (in addition to the TD1) from your employee, only use these tables on the portion of their income which is taxable with the claims information reported on the TD1

  • Do not use the tax tables

    • If the payment (for example, a bonus, a retroactive pay or other types of regular payments) is made separately from regular pay, use: Calculations of bonuses, retroactive payments or irregular amounts the bonus method to calculate manually
    • If the payment is for CPP contributions deductions in a single period, multiple periods or year-end verification, use: Manual calculations of CPP contributions deductions
    • If the payment is for EI premiums deductions in a single period, multiple periods or year-end verification, use: Manual calculations of EI premiums deductions
    • If the payment is for Income taxs deductions in a single period or multiple periods, use: Manual calculations of income tax deductions
    • If you received Form TD1X from your employee, use one of the following methods: Manual calculations of income tax deductions, Payroll Deductions Online Calculator (PDOC) or Formulas for employers using software programs
    • If you received Form TD3F from the individual, see Fishers and employment insurance and Self-employed Business, Professional, Commission, Farming, and Fishing Income

Determine the CPP contribution to withhold

The annual basic exemption is built into the CPP tables.

Learn more: Learn about CPP, the contribution rates, maximums and exemptions

  1. For current employees or pensioners, determine if you need to stop deducting CPP because the employee reached the maximum contribution for the year 

    Do not use the tax tables if they reached the maximum contribution for the year. To calculate, go to Manual calculations of CPP contributions deductions.

  2. Get the CPP contributions tables   that matches your pay period.

  3. Find the range that includes your employee's gross remuneration in the "Pay" column, include any taxable benefits

  4. Find the amount in the "CPP" column which is next to the the column "Pay"

  5. This amount is the CPP contribution that you should withhold from your employee's pay.

    You have to remit this amount and the same amount for your share of the CPP contribution.

Determine the EI premiums to withhold

You need to get the specific tax tables based on your determination of the province of employment, learn more: Determine the province of employment (POE)

Learn more: Learn about EI, the premium rates and maximums

  1. For current employees or pensioners, determine if you need to stop deducting EI because the employee reached the maximum premium for the year 

    Do not use the tax tables if they reached the maximum annual premium. To calculate, go to Manual calculations of EI premiums deductions.

  2. Get the EI premiums tables   that matches your pay period.

  3. Find the range that includes your employee's gross remuneration in the "Insurable earnings" column, include any taxable benefits

  4. Find the amount in the "Insurable premium" column which is next to the column "Insurable earnings"

  5. This amount is the EI premium that you should withhold from your employee's pay.

    You have to remit this amount and the same amount for your share of the EI premium.

Determine the federal, provincial and territorial income tax deductions tables

Learn more: Learn about income tax, the claim codes and the federal, provincial or territorial tax tables

  1. Get the Form TD1, Personal Tax Credits Return that was completed by your employee or your pensioner when they started their new job or they wanted to increase/reduce income tax deductions.

    Learn more: Get the completed TD1 forms

  2. Verify if the employee checked the box "Income from other employers or payers".

    If they checked the box on their TD1 forms that their expected employment income from all sources will be less than their total claim amount,do not deduct any federal or provincial tax. Do not use the tax tables.

    However, if you know that this statement is false, you must deduct federal and provincial tax from the salary: continue to step 2.

  3. Get the Federal, provincial and territorial income tax tables   that matches your pay period.

  4. Find the range that includes the "13. Total claim amount" (reported on your employee's or pensionner's Form TD1) in the "Total claim amount" column.

  5. Get the Federal, provincial and territorial claim codes   that matches your pay range which are next to the the column "Total claim amount".

  6. Find the amount of federal tax that you should withhold from your employee's pay

    If applicable, find the amount of provincial or territorial tax that you should withhold from your employee's pay

  7. Add the federal and provincial tax amounts to determine the total tax you deduct for the pay period.

    Even if the period of employment for which you pay a salary is less than a full pay period, you must continue to use the tax deductions table that corresponds to your regular pay period.

  8. These amounts are the federal, provincial and territorial income tax that you should withhold from your employee's pay.

    You have to remit these amounts.
