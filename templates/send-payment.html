Why you would send the [ABC] back

Your situation may have changed since you first applied for you may have made an honest mistake when applying. If we find that you are not eligible for the payments you receive, you will have to return them.

You must return or repay the [ABC] to the CRA if you:

  • applied for the [ABC] and later found that you are not eligible
  • received payments that you are not entitled to
  • received more than what you are entitled to

Periods that can be repaid

View each weekly period in a calendar

  • Period 1: August 30 to September 5, 2020
  • Period 2: September 6 to September 12, 2020
  • Period 3: September 13 to September 19, 2020

How to return the [ABC]

You can return or repay your [ABC] payment to the CRA online or by mail.

If you were paid by direct deposit or don't have the original [ABC] cheque

To repay the [ABC], choose one of the following options:

Online:
Sign in to CRA My Account
Online banking:

With your financial institution:

  1. Sign in to your financial institution's online banking service
  2. Under "Add a payee" look for an option such as: CRA (revenue) – tax instalment
  3. Enter your 9 digit social insurance number (SIN) as the CRA account number
By mail:

Mail a cheque or money order to the CRA:

  1. Make the payment out to "Receiver General for Canada"
  2. Indicate it is for "Repayment of [ABC]"
  3. Indicate which eligibility period you are repaying
  4. Include your Social Insurance Number (SIN) or your Temporary Tax Number (TTN)
  5. Mail your payment:

    Revenue Processing – Repayment of [ABC]
    Sudbury Tax Centre
    1050 Notre Dame Avenue
    Sudbury ON P3A 0C3

If you still have the original [ABC] cheque

You can return the cheque by mail.

Be sure to include:

  1. the reason for returning the cheque (not entitled or overpayment)
  2. your Social Insurance Number (SIN) or Temporary Tax Number (TTN)

Mail the cheque to:

Revenue Processing – Repayment of [ABC]
Sudbury Tax Centre
1050 Notre Dame Avenue
Sudbury ON P3A 0C3
