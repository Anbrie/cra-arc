Payroll correspondence you need to remit (pay)

You must use an original paper remittance voucher to make a payment at a Canadian financial institution.

Select the type of correspondence

Statement of account and current source deductions remittance voucher for regular and quarterly remitters (PD7A)

What is a PD7A

The PD7A is sent to regular and quarterly remitters.

The PD7A consists of both:

  • a statement of account for the previous remittances
  • a voucher for the next remittance

What to do with the PD7A

You must use the PD7A to remit (pay).

Learn more and view the sample: PD7A – Statement of account for current source deductions

Statement of account and current source deductions remittance voucher for accelerated remitters (PD7A(TM))

What is a PD7A(TM)

The PD7A(TM) allows you to make a payment if you discover that you have not remitted enough for a payroll period. The PD7A(TM) is sent to accelerated remitters. You will not receive a PD7A(TM) if you remit electronically.

The PD7A(TM) consists of both:

  • a statement of account
  • a remittance voucher

How often is a PD7A(TM) sent

The frequency of when the PD7A(TM) is sent depends on how often remittances are made. If you are an accelerated remitter who:

  • remits monthly, the PD7A(TM) is sent as soon as a payment is received. If a payment is not made, the PD7A(TM) is generally sent the 18th of the month.
  • does not remit monthly, the PD7A(TM) is sent quarterly, generally the 18th of the month following each calendar quarter (April, July, October, and January).

The PD7A-RB remittance voucher can also be used to remit if you are an accelerated remitter.

Remittance voucher booklets for accelerated remitters (PD7A-RB)

What is a PD7A-RB

The PD7A-RB is a remittance voucher booklet that is sent to accelerated remitters in December. You will not receive a PD7A-RB if you remit electronically or you received a PD7A(TM).

The PD7A-RB consists of either:

  • 27 vouchers per booklet for Threshold 1 remitters
  • 54 vouchers per booklet for Threshold 2 remitters
Remittance voucher for Payment on Filing (PoF) Source Deductions (PD7R)

What is a PD7R

The PD7R is a payment voucher that is generated as per your request. You can only request a PD7R if you are eligible to make a reconciliation payment by the last day of February without being subject to penalty or interest.

The PD7R cannot be used to remit current payroll remittances. You must use the PD7A for the current remittances.

Eligibility to use the PoF

Eligibility is applied to businesses separately. If you own multiple businesses, you may have businesses that are eligible and some that are not. If you use the PoF and you are not eligible, penalties and interest will be applied.

You are eligible to use PoF if you meet all of the following 3 criteria:

  • The reconciliation payment is less than 1% of the total annual remittances
  • Payroll compliance is perfect which means all of the following apply:
    • no late or outstanding remittances
    • no assessments in the filing year
    • all T4 information returns are filed on time
  • One or more of the following must apply:
    • You have employees who chose to be paid stock-based salaries or wages
    • You relied on third-party information for insurance, health benefits, broker information, taxable benefits and/or automobile fleet mileage
    • You have employees who live in other tax jurisdictions

Request for tax deductions, CPP and EI information if you remit late (PD1114)

What is a PD1114

The PD1114 is sent to you when a CPP, EI or income tax remittance is expected but not received.

How to respond to a PD1114 request

You must respond to the PD1114 request and provide one of the following explanation:

  • if a remittance has been made but not received by the CRA
  • the reason for not remitting a payment

You can send your response by:

  • MyBA or RAC (select "Respond to notices")
  • Fax number or the address provided on the PD1114 (using the Remittance breakdown form)
  • call us at 1-800-959-5525

What happens if you do not respond

If you do not respond to the PD1114 notice and you have not remitted deductions withheld at source, it is a serious offence.

Penalty for failure to deduct

We can assess a penalty of 10% of the amount of Canada Pension Plan (CPP), employment insurance (EI), and income tax you did not deduct.

If you are assessed this penalty more than once in a calendar year, we will apply a 20% penalty to the second or later failures if they were made knowingly or under circumstances of gross negligence.

Statement of account and partial payment for arrears balance remittance voucher (PD7D(AR))

What is a PD7D(AR)

The PD7D(AR) is sent to you when a partial payment for an arrears balance is received. Only use the remittance form attached to the statement to make that payment.

The PD7D(AR) consists of both:

  • a statement of account
  • a remittance voucher

The PD7D(AR) cannot be used to remit current payroll remittances. You must use the PD7A for the current remittances.

Statement of account and payment on existing balance remittance voucher (PD7D(X))

What is a PD7D(X)

The PD7D(X) is a notice that is generated as per your request. You can only request a PD7D(X) if there is an existing balance owing.

The PD7D(X) consists of both:

  • a statement of account
  • a remittance voucher that is used to pay an existing balance

If a partial payment is made on a balance owing, we will send you an updated Form PD7D, Notice of assessment with a new arrears remittance voucher and an updated balance including the interest.

The PD7D(X) cannot be used to remit current payroll remittances. You must use the PD7A for the current remittances.
