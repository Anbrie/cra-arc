Taxes and benefits for Indigenous peoples

As an Indigenous person, there may be some forms or processes specific to you. But in general, tax obligations are the same as any other resident in Canada.

Getting benefits and credits

There are some benefits that are only available if you file a tax return. So it’s worth doing even if you normally don’t have to

For example, the following benefits are available only once having filed:

  • GST/HST credit
  • Canada Child Benefit (CCB)
  • Disability Tax Credit
  • Northern Residents Deduction
  • Canada Workers Benefit

If you received COVID-related benefits

If you received COVID benefits, you may be able to claim taxes back, or you may owe additional taxes.

If you received the Canada Emergency Response Benefit (CERB)

If your regular income is tax-exempt and you received CERB, you don’t have to pay taxes on the CERB

It’s the income that led to your eligibility that determines if you’re eligible

Questions and answers about what is taxable in the CERB

If you do pay taxes on your income, you will have to pay taxes on the CERB.

Paying back CERB if necessary

If you received one of the recovery benefits (CRB, CRSB, CRCB)

Collecting 10% back if your income is tax-exempt. If you received one of the Canada Recovery Benefits 10% of the tax was withheld at source,

including those individuals with income that may be exempt from tax. If some or all of your income is exempt from tax under section 87 of the Indian Act and you are registered, or entitled to be registered as an Indian under that Act, you may be able to get part or all of the tax back for payments received in 2020 or 2021 by filing a tax return for that year. This may include filling out Form T90, Income exempt under the Indian Act, as part of the return or you may be eligible for a simple return (see the Getting Benefits and Credits section on this page)

If you earned more than $38,000 (other conditions) you will have to pay (something) back [use the Q and As that are being developed to answer this]

If your income is tax-exempt, you probably don’t have to pay it back.

CESB
