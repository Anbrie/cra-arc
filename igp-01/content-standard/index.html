Creating a user centred design - CRA instructional guidance product

Content is at the core of all of the products and services that CRA delivers. Follow this guidance to design useful, usable content for products and services across channels.

Content design always begins with research to understand user needs, so that you can determine the best way to deliver information to meet those needs. Content design is not limited to words; content can take many forms including: "pictures, diagrams, charts, links, calendars, a series of questions and answers, videos, addresses, maps, calculators, spreadsheets, printable documents" etc. (Richards, Content Design).

Sections

Start with user research
Whether you’re optimizing existing content, or creating new content, always begin with user research. Good content design should be informed by evidence and understanding of real user needs and context.
Design inclusive and accessible content
People with different knowledge, skills, experience, physical attributes, mental states, technological access, and literacy use CRA services. Plan content to be inclusive and accessible for all to reduce potential barriers.
