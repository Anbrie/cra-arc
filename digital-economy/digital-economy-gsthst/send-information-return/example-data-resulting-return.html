Examples of Digital Platform Information Returns

This page illustrate two examples in which Platform Operators use the DPIR schema to file information returns. The first example relates to sales of qualifying tangible personal properties, and the second example relates to rental of short-term accommodation.

Message Specification and Platform Operators data elements are common to both examples and are shown first.

Message Specification data element

Figure 1 shows a Message Specification data element, where the Platform Operator is located in the United States, it has a BN15 of “123456789RT0001”, and is filing an information return for the calendar year 2022. The information return was filed on April 17, 2023, at 9:30 am.

<dpir:MessageSpec>
 <dpir:SendingEntityIN>123456789RT0001</dpir:SendingEntityIN>
 <dpir:TransmittingCountry>US</dpir:TransmittingCountry>
 <dpir:MessageType>DPIR</dpir:MessageType>
 <dpir:Contact>John Valore: 1800-1010-0111 ext. 3458, 8am-4am EST.
 For more info: john.valore@digitalcart2050ee.com</dpir:Contact>
 <dpir:MessageRefId> US2022CADPIR123456789M10000101</dpir:MessageRefId>
 <dpir:MessageTypeIndic>DPI401</dpir:MessageTypeIndic>
 <dpir:ReportingPeriod>2022-12-31</dpir:ReportingPeriod>
 <dpir:Timestamp>2023-04-17T09:30:47Z</dpir:Timestamp>
</dpir:MessageSpec>

Figure 1: Example – Message Spec data element

The above message has a unique identifier "US2022CADPIR123456789M10000101" which follows the format specified in the DPIR schema user guide. The message identifier specifies the following:

  • The Platform Operator is located in the United States (“US”) and is filing an information return for the calendar year 2022 (“2022”)
  • The information return is filed and transmitted to Canada (“CA”), as part of the “DPIR” program
  • The Platform Operator's BN9 is "123456789"
  • The message root is "10000101"

Platform Operator data element

Figure 2 shows an example of a Platform Operator data element. 

<dpir:PlatformOperator URL="http://www.digitalcart2050ee.com/">
  <dpir:ResCountryCode>US</dpir:ResCountryCode>
  <dpir:TIN issuedBy="CA">123456789</dpir:TIN>
  <dpir:VAT>123456789RT0001</dpir:VAT>
  <dpir:Name>Cart 1</dpir:Name>
  <dpir:PlatformBusinessName>Digital Cart Universe</dpir:PlatformBusinessName>
  <dpir:Address legalAddressType="OECD303">
	<dpi:CountryCode>US</dpi:CountryCode>
	<dpi:AddressFix>
	  <dpi:Street>San Antonio Boulevard</dpi:Street>
	  <dpi:BuildingIdentifier>76200A</dpi:BuildingIdentifier>
	  <dpi:PostCode>92408</dpi:PostCode>
	  <dpi:City>San Bernardino</dpi:City>
	  <dpi:CountrySubentity>California</dpi:CountrySubentity>
	</dpi:AddressFix>
  </dpir:Address>
  <dpir:DocSpec>
    <stf:DocTypeIndic>OECD1</stf:DocTypeIndic>
	<stf:DocRefId> US2022CADPIR123456789M10000101000001</stf:DocRefId>
  </dpir:DocSpec>
</dpir:PlatformOperator>

Figure 2: Example – Platform Operator data element

Platform Operators will likely have both a BN9 (which is used as TIN) and a BN15 (which is used for a VAT), as Figure 2 shows. In the above example, the Platform operates under the business name "Digital Cart Universe", but is registered under the legal name "Cart 1". Figure 2 shows the URL of the Platform Operator.

The Platform Operator's business address (legalAddressType ='OECD303') is:

"76200A San Antonio Boulevard, San Bernardino, 92408, California, US".

Therefore, its tax residency country code is US.

The above example shows that the Document Reference ID of the Platform Operator is "US2022CADPIR123456789M10000101000001". The last six-digit message root ('10000101') number does not have to start from zero and can be any number as long as it is distinct from any other message root sent from the same Platform Operator for the same Reporting Period.

Sales of Qualifying Tangible Personal Properties

Consider an example data set containing transactional records of sales of qualifying tangible personal properties, which includes Seller’s details (both Individual Sellers and Entity Sellers), the provinces where the properties were sold, as well as the sales earned and the GST/HST collected. The example demonstrates the following:

  • Sellers can be either Individual Sellers or Entity Sellers.
  • Both Individual Sellers and Entity Sellers may have a BN9 and a BN15.
  • A Seller registered for GST/HST purposes will remit the taxes on its own.
  • Platform Operators report taxes and sales of Sellers that are not registered. They do not have to include taxes and sales of registered Sellers in the information return
  • Taxes are calculated and collected based on the province where the property is sold. Different provinces have different tax rates
  • The Platform Operator should always have a valid Province data element. A Province value of UNKNOWN may be used only in exceptional circumstances
  • Sales and taxes are aggregated at the Seller level, by Province and reported to the CRA

Rental of Short-Term Accommodation

This section illustrates an example of how to represent data on rental of short-term accommodation in XML using the DPIR schema.

For simplicity, we include only four Individual Sellers and do not show Identity data elements. In general, both Individual Sellers and Entity Sellers may be engaged in rental of short-term accommodation.

The example shows a breakdown of sales by property and rental duration. Property details, such as property address, do not have to be reported in the information return.

The example illustrates the following:

  • During a Reporting Period, a property may be rented on both a short-term basis (continuous rental duration of less than one month) and long-term basis (continuous duration of more than one month). Sales and taxes are aggregated separately for short-term rentals and long-term rentals, and only short-term totals are to be reported
  • Taxes charged on a property are based on the province where the property is located. For example, Seller 1 rents two properties: one property is located in Ontario (where the tax rate is 13%) and the other property is located in Alberta (where the tax rate is 5%)
  • Properties rented on long-term basis are not taxed
  • Platform Operators do not remit taxes on property rentals by Sellers who are registered for GST/HST. Seller 2 rents a property in Quebec (which has a tax rate of 5%), but the Platform Operator does not remit taxes on this rental because the individual is registered for GST/HST
  • Platform Operators report taxes and sales by province for Sellers that are not registered. They do not have to include taxes and sales of registered Sellers in the information return.
