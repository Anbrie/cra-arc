What are the income tax rates

Certain amounts that you deduct from the remuneration you pay an employee, as well as other authorized or claimed amounts, can reduce the amount of remuneration from which you deduct tax for the pay period.

Do not include the following amounts when calculating the remuneration:

  • A deduction for living in a prescribed zone, learn more: Housing and travel assistance benefits paid in a prescribed zone
  • An amount that a tax services office has authorized, learn more: Letter of authority
  • Employee's contributions to a registered pension plan (RPP), learn more: Contributions to a registered pension plan (RPP)
  • Union dues (for Quebec, see Guide TP-1015.G-V, Guide for Employers: Source Deductions and Contributions)
  • Employee's contributions to a retirement compensation arrangement (RCA) or certain pension plans, learn more: Retirement compensation arrangement
  • Employee's and employer's contributions to a registered retirement savings plan (RRSP) provided you have reasonable grounds to believe the employee can deduct the contribution for the year, learn more: RRSP contributions you withhold from remuneration
  • Employee's contributions to a pooled registered pension plan (PRPP), or a similar provincial pension plan, as long as the plan is registered with the Minister of National Revenue and you have reasonable grounds to believe the employee can deduct the contributions for the year, learn more: Pooled Registered Pension Plan (PRPP)
