The Digital Design and Production Directorate (DDPD) provides web and digital analytics to help partners understand how website visitors use Canada.ca (external link) and InfoZone.

Digital analytics is the process of gathering and analysing metrics to measure the behaviour of website visitors and identify trends. This helps determine the effectiveness and, where issues are identified, changes necessary to improve usability.

Data and analytics help partners with the following:

  • Determine how analytics can be used to develop Key Performance Indicators (KPI) to monitor the success of your content;
  • Determine how to improve webpages for a better user experience;
  • Determine how content is presently being used by visitors;
  • Determine what content is used and and/or not being used;
  • Determine the most popular path(s) taken by users.

Web campaigns are planned series of communication products designed to get specific messages across to intranet users. They have defined objectives to help evaluate and track their successes and failures.

Before developing any communication product, please contact your PAB communications advisor. They will provide you with advice regarding your product and may draft a communications strategy for your project. DDPD’s Data and Usability Analysis (DUA) can help track and report web campaign progress.

By creating a tracking schedule and dashboards, DUA can show you how to monitor and track your web campaign. Dashboards summarize large quantities of data into easy-to-comprehend visuals, such as graphs and tables. This makes it much easier to present key information and to track how close you are to completing goals. It is easy to modify a dashboard to accommodate new information, providing you with the flexibility to adjust the dashboard at any point.

DUA can help by analyzing previous campaigns. DUA can help you determine the campaign's look, as well as its objectives. By using various metrics, DUA will turn the collected data into a cohesive story.

Steps to track web campaigns

Step
1 Analyze past web campaigns

  • Examine any past web campaigns that relate to a similar topic
    • This will help you understand potential successes, failures, or opportunities
  • It is important to analyze how past web campaigns were planned, executed and tracked in order to uncover areas of concern

Step
2 Determine business goals and user needs

  • Create a list of business goals and user needs for the web campaign to accomplish. Consult this link for more information: Content will meet specific business and user needs while considering impact on the whole organization
    • The goals should be specific, measurable and achievable within a reasonable amount of time
  • Schedule a meeting with the Digital Design and Production Directorate (DDPD) before developing the web content to discuss these goals.
  • Examples of business goals and user needs:
    • Improve communications with headquarters, and the regions through one central program office
    • Establish national service standards
    • Develop and deliver online training courses
    • Implement self-service practices

Step
3 Define how to measure goals

  • Create a strategy and timeline of what you want the web campaign to accomplish
  • Create measurable parameters to monitor the web campaign
  • Determine what defines a success or failure
  • Examples of measures:
    • Receive a certain number of page views
    • Increase page views by a certain percentage
    • Reduce the number of phone calls from clients
    • Reduce the amount of clicks required to navigate to desired content

Step
4 Record and monitor website usage

  • Ensure you record all important dates and times of activities that can affect the web campaign
  • Keep track of important dates:
    • Launch dates
    • Any communication to employees (e.g. emails to different audiences)
  • Ensure that at least one person is responsible for monitoring the web campaign on InfoZone and/or Canada.ca (external link)
    • DUA can create custom reports to help employees keep track of the web campaign

Step
5 Discuss lessons learned from the web campaign

  • Wait until there is sufficient information collected to give an overall representation of the web campaign
  • Analyze the information to understand its successes and failures
