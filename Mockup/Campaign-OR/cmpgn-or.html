Purpose

Use to provide information and promote campaigns. Use to promote topic awareness, only if the content is temporary, and is created specifically for the campaign and does not exist on the topic it promotes. Otherwise, promote awareness in a temporary notice on the topic page directly.

Design

Placement of elements and wording of standard headings in the templates are pre-determined and cannot be changed, unless otherwise specified. Do not use this page type for any other content without the approval of Design/IA.

Pattern

Campaign main page pattern

Governance

Adhere to the following:

  • Ensure headings and link text are parallel
  • Use Teaser format for description text
  • Refer to Intranet naming conventions: Campaign for further label information
  • When using a banner, use the alternative text to name the campagin alt="Banner: Campaign name"
  • Add the following code for links to any campaign contact page <a href="/english/..."><span class="wb-inv">Contacts - </span>(Position titles)<span class="wb-inv"> - (Campaign name)</span></a>
    • Follow the governance for headings for all contact pages
    • The position titles are not required in the H1 as long as the titles are present somewhere on the page

Life cycle

To help reduce redundant, outdated, and trivial (ROT) content, and improve the users' search experience, adhere to the following:

  • Ensure campaigns are temporary and have a short life cycle
  • Remove all campaign pages no later than one week after the campaign ends
    • This includes any initiative banner on branch or regional pages which references the campaign from the intranet
    • Exception: For national campaigns, the main, national campaign page can remain
      • Update content to reflect that the campaign is finished, as well as provide a summary of results
  • Communicate local area campaign results to employees in a News item on their local Community bulletin boards
  • Replace prior annual year's campaign information with current information, once the new campaign marketing begins
    • Add a brief blurb to the new campaign page if there is a need to reference past year's campaign
  • Do not use the intranet as a storage space; it is for current information that meets the needs of the majority
    • If you are legally responsible to keep specific information for longer periods of time, or need it for internal reporting purposes, store it on your shared drive in accordance with the requirements established for Campaigns, canvassing and soliciting

Indexing/appearance in search results

Adhere to the following:

  • Index only pages like the CRA Charitable Campaign main page in Priorities and initiatives (same approach applies to other national campaigns)
  • Do not index individual campaign pages for specific branches and regions pages, as users can find them on their Community bulletin board pages
  • Do not index all content
    • Not every page of content needs to appear in search results
    • It is impossible to find desired information when every page is indexed, as an endless listing of search results appears
    • When a limited number of webpages on a specific topic are indexed, users can easily find what they need from the reduced options
    • Users navigate to non-indexed content via links in the indexed content (located through search)
