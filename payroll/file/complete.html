What are payroll information returns

Payroll information returns contain slips and a summary. Depending on the type of income you pay, you may have to complete and issue more than one type of return.

Types of payroll information returns

Select the type of information return to find out who must complete, what to report and when to issue slips.

T4 - Information for employers

Who needs to complete a T4 information return

Generally, you need to complete a T4 slip if you are an employer (resident or non-resident) and you paid your employees employment income, commissions, taxable allowances and benefits, fishing income, or any other remuneration.

What to report on a T4 slip

You must report the following remuneration on a T4 slip:

  • Salary or wages
  • Tips or gratuities
  • Bonuses
  • Vacation pay
  • Employment commissions
  • Gross and insurable earnings of self-employed fishers
  • Taxable benefits or allowances
  • Retiring allowances
  • Deductions withheld during the year
  • Pension adjustment (PA) amounts for employees who accrued a benefit for the year under your registered pension plan (RPP) or deferred profit sharing plan (DPSP)

Do not report on a T4 slip

Do not report the following on a T4 slip:

  • If you paid pensions, lump-sum payments, annuities, or other income (including amounts paid to a proprietor or partner of an unincorporated business), see T4A – Information for payers
  • If you paid amounts from a retirement compensation agreement, see T4A - Retirement Compensation Arrangement (RCA) trust
  • If you paid fees (except for director fees), commissions, or other amounts to a non-resident for services rendered in Canada, other than employment situations, see 4A - Payments to non-residents for services provided in Canada
  • If you are an employer with construction as your primary source of business income, see T1204 - Government services contract payments or T5018 - Statement of contract payments

When to issue a T4 slip

You must issue a T4 slip if any of the following apply:

  • you were required to deduct CPP/QPP, EI, PPIP, or income tax from remuneration paid to your employee
  • if not part of the exception, the remuneration paid to your employee was more than $500 in the calendar year (under the CRA administrative policy)

Exception

If you provide current employees with taxable group term life insurance benefits, you always have to issue T4 slips, even if the total of all remuneration paid in the calendar year is $500 or less. If you provide former employees or retirees with such benefits, you have to issue a T4A slip.
