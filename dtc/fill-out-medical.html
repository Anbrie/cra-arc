Certify the form by a medical professional

The Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) makes a decision about your patient's eligibility for the disability tax credit (DTC) based on the information given on Part B of Form T2201.

Before you start

Step 1 Use the correct form

Use the most recent version of Form T2201, Disability Tax Credit Certificate, to make sure you are providing relevant information.

Step 2 Make sure you can fill out the applicable section for your patient

The following table shows the type of medical practitioner who can certify each section.

Sections each medical practitioner can certify
Medical practitioner: can certify:
Medical doctor all sections
Nurse practitioner all sections
Optometrist vision
Audiologist hearing
Occupational therapist walking, feeding, dressing, and the cumulative effect for these activities
Physiotherapist walking
Psychologist mental functions necessary for everyday life
Speech-language pathologist speaking

Charging fees

Any fees that the medical practitioner charges to fill out the form or to give us more information can be claimed a medical expenses on line 330 or line 331 of a tax return.

Proposed rules

We published draft regulations that would limit the amount of fees that can charge for preparing DTC claims to $100. Refer to: Questions and answers to the proposed Disability Tax Credit Promoters Restrictions Regulations

Fill out Part B of Form T2201

Step 1 Check the patient's name is on every page

Make sure your patient's name is at the top of each page.

Step 2 Fill out the applicable section for your patient

Fill out only the section or sections that apply to your patient on page 2 to 4 on Form T2201. To learn more about the criteria and to see examples for each sections, see Eligibility criteria for the disability tax credit.

Use your best professional judgment when you fill out Part B of Form T2201 for your patient.

Make your assessment based on the following information:

  • the symptoms reported by the patient
  • your knowledge of the patient's medical history
  • your knowledge of effects of the impairment(s) as they relate to your patient's medical condition

Make sure you answer all the questions in the applicable sections of Form T2201.

For more information about the DTC, call 1-800-959-8281, between the hours of 9:00 a.m. and 5:00 p.m., Eastern Standard Time.

Step 3 Fill out the mandatory sections

There are three mandatory sections for you to fill out on page 5.

Effects of impairment

Eligibility for the DTC is based on the effects of the impairment, not on the medical condition itself. Provide sufficient detail to demonstrate how the patient's impairment functionally impacts their ability to do one (two or more in the case of cumulative effects) of the applicable basic activities of daily living, are blind or need for life-sustaining therapy.

Although a diagnosis may be useful in helping us make a decision on eligibility, it is not, in itself, not enough information.

Duration

Indicate if your patient's impairment lasted or is reasonably expected to last for a continuous period of at least 12 months. Answer the question about whether the impairment is likely to improve to such an extent that your patient would no longer be markedly restricted, significantly restricted, or in need for life-sustaining therapy. If the impairment is expected to improve, indicate the year it might occur.

Certification

Answer the questions and provide your contact information. You must also certify that the information in Part B is correct and complete.

After you fill out the form

Medical professional sends in form: Option 1 of 2

You can send Form T2201 to your patient's tax centre. If you need to send us a letter, you can send it to the same address.

Patient or representative sends in form: Option 2 of 2

Your patient or their representative can send Form T2201 to your patient's tax centre.

If the information provided is insufficient or contradictory, we will contact you in writing for clarification.

We will review the information you provide to determine your patient's eligibility for the DTC. We will advise your patient of our decision concerning their eligibility.
