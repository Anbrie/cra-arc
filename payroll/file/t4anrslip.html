What to report and not report

  • Report: Option 1

    • Amounts paid to non-resident individuals, partnerships, and corporations for services they performed in Canada that they did not perform in the ordinary course of an office or employment

  • Do not report: Option 2

    • If you paid non-resident employees in Canada who are in regular and continuous employment in Canada and who provide employment services in Canada on a temporary or permanent basis use: T4 slips - Information for employers
    • item
    • If you paid directors' fees to a non-resident director use: T4 slips - Information for employers
    • If you paid amounts for acting services of a non-resident actor in a film, television, or video production rendered in Canada. Report those amounts on a NR4 slip (see Non-resident actors)

What are the guidelines for filling out slips

  • Do: Option 1

    • If you have multiple payroll accounts, file a T4A-NR return for each payroll account
    • Amounts are reported on a T4A-NR slip for the year in which it is paid
    • Report, in dollars and cents, all amounts you paid during the year, except pension adjustment amounts, which are reported in dollars only
    • Report all amounts in Canadian dollars, even if they were paid in another currency, learn more on the average exchange rates: Exchange rates

  • Do not: Option 2

    • Do not show negative dollar amounts on slips, to make changes to previous years, see Make corrections after filing
    • If a box does not have a value, do not enter "nil" or "N/A", leave the box blank
    • Do not change the headings of any of the boxes
    • Do not enter hyphens or dashes between numbers
    • Do not enter the dollar sign ($)
