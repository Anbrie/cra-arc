Determine which tax tables to use

When employers or payers pay employment income such as salaries, wages, or commissions, the individuals's province or territory of employment (POE) must be determined so that the proper income tax deductions are withheld. This depends on whether the individual physically (in person) reports at the employer's or payer's establishment.

CRA's mandate - Administering provincial and territorial personal income tax

CRA's mandate is to administer provincial and territorial personal income tax as legislated by the province or territory. Any concerns regarding the tax policy of a province or territory should be directed to the applicable provincial or territorial Ministry of Finance.

What is considered the establishment of the employer or payer

An establishment of the employer or payer is any place or premises in Canada that is owned, leased or rented by an employer or payer where individuals report to work or from which individuals are paid. For purposes of province of employment, this does not need to be a permanent establishment.

Generally, a home office will not be considered an establishment of the employer or payer.

Step 1 - Determine the residency status

The individual, employer or payer may be uncertain of their residency status.

Residency status is determined on a case by case basis and an individual's whole situation. All relevant facts provided by the taxpayer to the CRA in NR73, Determination of residency status (leaving Canada) and NR74, Determination of residency status (entering Canada) must be considered. This includes looking at their residential ties with Canada, their intentions and the length of time, purpose and continuity of stays in Canada or abroad. For more information, see Determining your residency status.

Learn more about the residency status of an individual: Individuals – Leaving or entering Canada and non-residents

Learn more about the residency status of an employer or payer: Businesses – International and non-resident taxes

How to ask for a binding determination

To obtain certainly and require a binding determination about the tax consequences of the proposed departure from or arrival in Canada, a person may ask the Income Tax Rulings Directorate (ITRD) to issue a binding advance income tax ruling with respect to the residency status of the person. The taxpayer may have to pay a fee to have ITRD complete such a request. For more information on how to ask for an advance income tax ruling, see Information Circular IC 70-6R9, Advance Income Tax Rulings and Technical Interpretations or Income tax rulings and interpretations.

Situation: Income is self-employment earnings - Person is a resident of Canada

Use the Provincial or territorial table of the self-employment recipient's province or territory of residence.

Explanation

The withholding of income tax to be applied is based on the province of residence of the self-employed recipient.

Act

  • ITR: 100(4)(b)
Situation: Income is self-employment earnings - Person is a non-resident of Canada

The withholding of income tax to be applied is 15% for the self-employed non-resident recipient.

Explanation

The withholding of tax is applicable to advance payments made in respect of services to be performed in Canada and must be deducted at the time of payment unless a Regulation 105 waiver was obtained. For more information, see IC75-6R2, Required withholding from amounts paid to non-residents providing services in Canada.

Non-residents waivers

  • Non-residents in these situations may request for a Regulation 105 waiver.
  • Non-resident artists and athletes may use a simplified waiver application.
