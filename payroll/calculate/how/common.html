CPP, EI and income tax deductions tables

Information on how to calculate and to determine the payroll deductions for amounts you pay your employees in common pay periods.

On this page

  • Determine the CPP contribution to withhold
  • Determine the EI premiums to withhold
  • Determine the federal and provincial income tax to withhold
  • References

Determine the CPP contribution to withhold

The annual basic exemption is built into the CPP tables.

  1. Open one of the following CPP contributions tables that matches your pay period:

    • Common pay periods: Option 1

      • 52 pay periods a year (Weekly) [PDF]
      • 26 pay periods a year (Biweekly) [PDF]
      • 24 pay periods a year (Semi-monthly) [PDF]
      • 12 pay periods a year (Monthly) [PDF]

    • Uncommon pay periods: Option 2

    • Weekly: 53 pay periods a year
    • Biweekly: 27 pay periods a year
    • 22 pay periods a year
    • 13 pay periods a year
    • 10 pay periods a year

  2. Find the range that includes your employee's gross remuneration in the "Pay" column, include any taxable benefits

  3. Find the amount in the "CPP" column which is next to the the column "Pay"

  4. This amount is the CPP contribution that you should withhold from your employee's pay.

Determine the EI premiums to withhold

You need to get the specific tax tables based on your determination of the province of employment, learn more: Determine the province of employment (POE)

  • Open one of the following EI premiums tables that matches your employee's province of employment:
    • Employment insurance (EI) premiums tables [PDF] , other than the province of Quebec
    • Quebec employment insurance (EI) premiums tables [PDF]
  • Find the range that includes your employee's gross remuneration in the "Insurable earnings" column, include any taxable benefits
  • Find the amount in the "Insurable premium" column which is next to the column "Insurable earnings"

This amount is the EI premium that you should withhold from your employee's pay.

Determine the federal and provincial income tax to withhold

You need to get the specific tax tables based on your determination of the province of employment, learn more: Determine the province of employment (POE)

To determine your employees' and pensioners' total tax deductions, you have to look up the amounts in both the federal tax table and the provincial tax table. To determine the total tax you deduct for the pay period, you must add the federal and provincial tax amounts.

Even if the period of employment for which you pay a salary is less than a full pay period, you must continue to use the tax deductions table that corresponds to your regular pay period.

Federal tax deductions tables

  • Find the pages in Section D that correspond to your pay period
  • To find the range that corresponds to your employee's taxable income (this includes any taxable benefits), look down the "Pay" column
  • In the row under the applicable claim code, you will find the amount of federal tax that you should withhold from your employee's pay (for more information, go to the section called "Claim codes" and Chart 3)

Provincial tax deductions tables

  • Find the pages in Section E that correspond to your pay period
  • To find the range that includes your employee's taxable income (this includes any taxable benefits), look down the "Pay" column
  • In the row under the applicable claim code, you will find the amount of provincial tax that you should withhold from your employee's pay (for more information, go to the section called "Claim codes" and Chart 4)
