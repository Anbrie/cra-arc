Law of focal point

On this page

  • Overview
  • Examples
  • Take aways

Overview

Definition: Whatever stands out visually (a point of interest, emphasis, or difference) is first to capture and hold the viewer's attention.

In practice: It is the first point of interest that grabs the user. The user then moves to other parts of the page. With the focal point, determine where it is, how many there are, and does it emphasize the correct thing.
