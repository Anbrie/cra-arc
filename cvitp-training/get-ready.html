Roles and responsibilities

CVITP volunteers can help eligible taxpayers prepare their taxes by participating as a tax preparer, a coordinator, or in a support role. Depending on the community organization you are volunteering with, you may be assigned one or more of the following roles:

  • Tax preparer
  • Coordinator
  • Support Roles:
    • Greeter
    • Interpreter
    • Screener
    • Administrator

Before starting the training, review the roles and responsibilities you will assume as a CVITP volunteer.

Register to volunteer

To participate as a CVITP volunteer for the upcoming tax season, ensure that you have met all of the requirements for your role(s).

  • Affiliate yourself with a CVITP organization (your CRA coordinator may be able to help you find one)
  • Register as a CVITP volunteer (annual registration)
  • Provide a valid police records check* (PRC) to your organization if you are:
    • accessing taxpayer information
    • preparing and submitting paper returns
    • using Auto-fill my return (AFR)

Tip

Contact your organization to confirm if you need to provide a valid police records check.

Additionally, if you are a volunteer tax preparer, you may also need to:

  • register for an EFILE number, to electronically file tax returns (initial registration or annual renewal)
  • obtain a RepID (one-time registration if you plan to use Auto-fill my return)

For more information on the different requirements, refer to Register to volunteer.

Important reminder for returning volunteers

Complete the online CVITP registration form, found under Register to volunteer, and the annual renewal of your EFILE account.

Getting access the tax software

A new version of UFile CVITP software is released each year.

If you intend to have the software installed on your personal laptop for use at CVITP clinics, your organization or CRA coordinator will email you the download link and UFile activation key.

If you need to install the UFile CVITP tax software for prior tax years, your organization or CRA coordinator can provide you the required software and activation keys.

Your organization may also provide you with a CVITP Organization Identification Number (COIN). This number is used tcount the number of federal income tax and benefit returns electronically filed by your organization through UFile CVITP.

For more information refer to EFILE-Netfile setup under Setting up the software.

Supplies and logistics

To ensure you can efficiently perform your role, we suggest the following:

Recommended supplies:

  • Desktop or laptop computer
  • Internet connection
  • Forms TIS60 - CVITP Taxpayer Authorization (mandatory) and CVITP pre-populated Authorization request – signature page (if using Auto-fill my return)
  • UFile activation key
  • EFILE number and password

Useful supplies:

  • Printer and USB flash drive
  • Federal Income Tax and Benefit Guide
  • Forms, such as the T1 Adjustment request (T1-ADJ), Direct deposit enrollment (8001-522E), Address change request (RC325) and Disability Tax Credit Certificate (T2201)
  • Pre-addressed tax centre envelopes
  • Pencils, paper, calculator, stapler, and other general office supplies

Forms commonly used by CVITP volunteers can be accessed and printed directly from the tools and references section. For information on how to order forms online or over the telephone, refer to useful contacts.

Virtual clinic supplies

If you file tax returns virtually, be prepared to have:

  • a telephone/cell phone
  • an email address
  • a tablet or other smart device

Logistics:

  • Time of arrival (before the start of your shift)
  • Duration of appointments (time scheduled for each appointment)

Tip

To ensure you have sufficient time tset up your workstation, arrive at least 30 minutes before the start of your shift.

Check with your host organization to see if they can accommodate extra time for you to prepare.
