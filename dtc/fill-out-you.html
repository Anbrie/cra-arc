Fill out the form for yourself or as a representative

On this page

  • Get Form T2201
  • Fill out Part A of Form T2201
  • Ask a medical practitioner to fill out and certify Part B of Form T2201

Step 1 Get Form T2201

Use the most recent version of Form T2201, Disability Tax Credit Certificate, to make sure you are providing relevant information.

Step 2 Fill out Part A of Form T2201

Part A has to be filled out by the person with the disability (or a legal representative). Fill out only the sections of Part A that apply to you.

Adjusting your previous tax returns

In most cases, the CRA can, once eligibility is approved, adjust your tax return(s) for all applicable years to include the disability amount for yourself or your dependant under the age of 18. If you want the CRA to adjust your tax returns, tick the "Yes" box in Section 3 of Part A.

Fees

The Disability Tax Credit Promoters Restrictions Regulations Act (DTCPRA) is proposed legistation that sets a maximum fee of $100 for helping you fill out the form, plus sets a maximum of $100 per year for a reassessment request. The Act is not law yet.

Refer to: Questions and answers to the proposed Disability Tax Credit Promoters Restrictions Regulations

Step 3 Ask a medical practitioner to fill out and certify Part B of Form T2201

Part B has to be filled out by the medical practitioner. Take the form to a medical practitioner who can certify the sections that apply.

Which medical practitioner can certify each section
Medical practitioner can certify:
Medical doctor all sections
Nurse practitioner   all sections
Optometrist vision
Audiologist hearing
Occupational therapist walking, feeding, dressing, and the cumulative effect for these activities
Physiotherapist walking
Psychologist performing the mental functions necessary for everyday life
Speech-language pathologist speaking

Fees

You are responsible for any fees that the medical practitioner charges to fill out the form or to give us more information. However, you may be able to claim these fees as medical expenses on line 330 or line 331 of your tax return.
