When to file

To maintain your charitable registration, you must file a complete T3010 return no later than 6 months after the end of your fiscal period.

We recommend signing up for My Business Account (MyBA) as early as possible to be able to file your return online.

How to file

Through MyBA

If you have never used our digital services before, go to My Business Account for charities for information on how to create an account and start filing your T3010 return.

To file a return online, sign into MyBA and then select "File a return".

When you file your T3010 return online, MyBA identifies all necessary forms, sections, and schedules you need to complete, and reminds you to attach required documentation.

Since the forms are interactive, MyBA will only generate relevant schedules and appendices based on your previously selected answers.

MyBA helps to ensure you have submitted a complete T3010 return based on your charity's situation.

By mail

If you are not able to file a complete T3010 return online, you can contact the Charities Directorate to requests a paper form.

When you file a paper T3010 return, you must include all of the following forms and documents:

  • Form T3010, Registered Charity Information Return (the most updated version)
  • Form T1235, Directors/Trustees and Like Officials Worksheet
  • Form T1236, Qualified donees worksheet / Amounts provided to other organizations (if applicable)
  • Form T2081, Excess corporate holding worksheet for private foundations (if applicable)
  • Financial statements (even if your charity was inactive)

When filing your T3010 return, make sure your public contact information is still valid. This allows the public and the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) to communicate with your charity.

Mail the completed return and all required documentation to:

Charities Directorate
Canada Revenue Agency
105 – 275 Pope Road
Summerside PE C1N 6E8

When your return is mailed, the postmark date indicated on the envelope is used as the received date.

If you mail your complete T3010 return to an address other than the Charities Directorate (such as a tax services office), processing will be delayed.

For more information on how to complete your T3010 return, refer to Guide T4033, Completing Form T3010 Registered Charity Information Return.

If your charity is incorporated in Ontario

If your charity is incorporated in Ontario and subject to the Ontario Not-for-profit Corporations Act, you must file an Ontario annual information return. For more information, visit Ontario Business Registry.

If your charity was inactive

A charity is inactive if, during the entire fiscal year, it did not use any of its resources to carry out its charitable activities or to further its charitable purposes.

If your charity was inactive for a fiscal year, you must still file a complete T3010 return and explain why you were inactive in Section C: Question C2.

If you do not file your return, you risk losing your charitable status.

If you remain inactive, we may review your file to see if you still qualify for registration.

If your charity is no longer operating

Even if your charity is no longer operating, you must still file an annual T3010 return.

If your charity is no longer operating, you should ask for voluntary revocation of your registration. Once revocation is granted, you will no longer have to file a T3010 for any future fiscal period.

For more information, visit Request voluntary revocation.

If your charity made special requests

Some requests need approval by the CRA and can affect your return.

  • Fiscal year end change: If you received approval to change your fiscal year-end, you will need to file a separate return for the months between your old fiscal year-end and the beginning of your new fiscal year.
  • Permission to accumulate funds: If you received approval to accumulate funds, report the amount accumulated during the fiscal year, including any income earned, on line 5500 and any accumulated funds spent on line 5510.
  • Disbursement quota reduction: If you received approval for a disbursement quota reduction, you must file Form T1240, Registered Charity Adjustment Request, to correct the return for the fiscal year the shortfall occurred in.

If you fail to submit your return

If you do not file your return within 6 months of the end of your fiscal year we will start the revocation process for failure to file.

We will send you a T2051A, Notice of Intention to Revoke a Charity's Registration, by registered mail. You have 45 days from the date of this notice to file your return and 90 days to file an objection.

If you do not file an objection or appeal, or if it was unsuccessful, your charitable registration will be revoked. We will send you, by registered mail, a T2051B, Notice of Revocation of a Charity's Registration, which states the effective date of revocation.

If your charity's registration is revoked, you:

  • can no longer issue official donation receipts
  • are no longer exempt from paying income tax
  • must give all of your remaining assests to an eligible donee or pay a revocation tax equal to the full value of your assets

After you file

After you file your T3010 return, we will send you a confirmation of annual information return filing.

The information on your return will be available on the List of charities. You are responsible for making sure that your return is correct. If you notice an error, make sure to amend your return. Do not file another return for the same year.

How to amend your charity's return

Through MyBA

If you have never used our digital services before, go to My Business Account for charities for information on how to create an account and amend your return online.

To amend a return online,sign into MyBA and then select "Adjust a return".

If you need help accessing MyBA or have questions about amending your return online, you can contact the Charities Directorate.

By mail

If you are not able to amend your return online, you can file a paper version.

Amend your T3010 return

Complete Form T1240, Registered Charity Adjustment Request for each T3010 return that you need to correct.

Make sure you enter the fiscal year-end on each form.

The form must be signed by a representative of your charity that the Charities Directorate has on file.

Amend other forms filed with your T3010 return

Complete Form T1240, Registered Charity Adjustment Request and attach an amended form (for example, Form T1235 or Form T1236).

Keep copies of the Form T1240 and any revised forms with your books and records.

You can send the form by mail to:

Charities Directorate
Canada Revenue Agency
