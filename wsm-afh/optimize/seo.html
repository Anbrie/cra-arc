As creators and users of CRA content on Canada.ca, we all have an important role to play in optimizing for search!

What is SEO? SEO refers to search engine optimization, or the process of optimizing a website or webpages in order to make it easy to find information via search engines like Google, or on Canada.ca

The basics of well optimized search experience are the same for every search engine, however there are differences to note for external search engines, such as Google, and on site search on Canada.ca.

Here's what you need to know and how you can help.

Canada.ca content creators

  • Create page titles that are unique, accurate, that read naturally and effectively communicates the topic of the page content. Page titles are shown in search engine result pages (SERP), therefore a well written and engaging page title can increase click through rates from search engine result pages.
  • Focus each page on a single topic, using targeted keywords/phrases and plain language that users utilize. Consult the Content style guide – Canada.ca (external link) to create quality content for the web with emphasis on Writing principles for web content (external link).
  • Avoid putting redundant, outdated and trivial (ROT) in the page content. This helps the search engine retrieve information that is up to date, relevant, and authoritative.
  • Consult the Writing metadata - Canada.ca and prepare useful, effective and required metadata.
  • Test keywords and phrases to see how your pages are ranking in search results.
  • Use web analytics services to help understand how your content is being used. This will help you make decisions on what to keep or delete, how to address the content gap or improve ranking of your web content.
  • Work with a Search specialist on improving findability of your content on Google as well as on Canada.ca search. Start a conversation by submitting a SEO services request.

Improve InfoZone search results

As users and creators of InfoZone content, we all have an important role to play in improving the InfoZone search results! Here's how you can help.

InfoZone search users

  • Review the InfoZone search tips
  • Explore the advanced search options.
  • Use specific keywords related to the content you are searching for
  • Provide feedback or communicate with the SEO services team by submitting Feedback about this web page on InfoZone search page.

InfoZone content creators

Search engine findability tips:

  • Create page titles that are unique and clearly describe the content of the page.
  • Take the time to write useful and effective intranet metadata. Don't forget that your title and description metadata tell users about your page in the search results!
  • Test keywords and phrases to see how your pages are ranking in search results.
  • Seek feedback from your audiences to learn what improvements are needed.
  • Use web analytics services to help understand how your content is being used. This will help you make decisions on what to keep or delete.
  • Work with your Enterprise Search specialist on improving findability of your content in InfoZone search. Start a conversation by submitting a Search engine optimization (SEO) services request.

Content planning tips:

  • Focus each page on a single topic.
  • Have a content strategy in place to better manage and optimize intranet content.
  • Consult the Content creation principles (external link) and follow rules of creating quality content for the web.
  • Avoid putting redundant, outdated and trivial (ROT) content on InfoZone. This helps the search engine retrieve information that is timely, relevant, and from the authoritative source (for more information see our page on Intranet ROT reduction).
  • Plan your regular content audits to ensure good content management throughout content lifecycle.

Learning tips:

  • Log on to GCcampus (external link) and take a course on how to write effective intranet content called "Fundamentals of Writing for the Web (A313)".
  • Take a course on metadata called "Writing Metadata for the CRA Intranet (TD3503-000)".
